## Activities by Stop/Day

### Day 1 — 2026-07-04 (arrival 11:54, usable ~12:15–20:00)

**1. Upper Town & Trinity Hill (Verkhni Horad / Troitskoye Predmestye) walk**
- Type: Culture (free open-air historic quarter)
- Duration: ~1.5 hours
- Cost: **0 BYN** (free to walk; optional cafe stop is a food-agent item)
- Location/access: ~15–20 min walk from Ploshcha Lyenina metro (station exit), or one stop to Kastrychnickaya/Kupalauskaya then a short walk. Compact, flat, pedestrian-only cobblestone streets.
- Family suitability: Short, flat, stroller/kid-pace walk past colorful restored 19th-century buildings, the Holy Spirit Cathedral exterior, and the riverside — easy for a 5-year-old, visually interesting for an 8-year-old, no ticket/queue stress. No indoor accessibility issue since it's outdoor and level.
- Source: [In Your Pocket – Troitskoye Predmestye](https://www.inyourpocket.com/minsk/Troitskoye-Predmestye-Trinity-Suburb_2472v), [Free Attractions in Minsk 2026](https://allinmap.app/en/city-guides/free-attractions-minsk)

**2. National History Museum of the Republic of Belarus**
- Type: Culture (museum) — **satisfies the museum/culture interest requirement**
- Duration: ~1–1.5 hours (curated, kid-paced visit rather than full collection)
- Cost: Open daily 11:00–19:00, at 12 Karl Marx St, ~5–10 min walk from Upper Town. **Assumption (explicit, unchanged from v1): exact 2026 ticket price not confirmed by search** (museum's own price list page did not surface a figure); using a typical Belarusian state-museum price band as a placeholder: **adult ≈ 8 BYN × 2 = 16 BYN, child (8yo) ≈ 4 BYN, child (5yo, likely free under a young-child threshold) ≈ 0 BYN → family estimate ≈ 20 BYN**. Downstream budget-agent should treat this as a rough placeholder pending confirmation at histmuseum.by.
- Location/access: 12 Karl Marx St, central Minsk; walkable from Upper Town or one metro stop from Ploshcha Lyenina/Kastrychnickaya.
- Family suitability: Central, short-visit-friendly museum; recommend targeting 1–2 kid-interesting halls (e.g., ancient/medieval artifacts, WWII-era section) rather than the full 500,000-item collection, to avoid museum fatigue for a 5-year-old. Indoor, air-conditioned, no major accessibility barriers noted.
- Source: [National History Museum of the Republic of Belarus – official (EN)](https://history.museum.by/en), [Belarusian National History Museum – Belarus.travel](https://en.belarus.travel/landmarks/belarusian-national-history-museum)

**3. Gorky Park (Minsk Central Children's Park named after Gorky)**
- Type: Outdoor/park + amusement rides — **satisfies the park/nature interest requirement**
- Duration: ~2 hours
- Cost: Park entry itself is free; pay-per-ride. Individual rides ≈ **1–5 BYN each**; Ferris wheel ≈ 15–20 BYN per cabin (cabin, not per person — pricing varies by source and should be re-verified locally). **v2 planning figure (per iteration-plan-v1 guidance): adopt the LOW END of the previously stated range → 15 BYN total for the family, covering roughly 3 shared rides, skipping the Ferris wheel.** (v1 had used a ~15–20 BYN band; the recommended planning figure is now fixed at the low end, 15 BYN, not the midpoint.)
- Location/access: Near Nyamiha metro station (M1 line) or a ~20-min walk from Upper Town along the river.
- Family suitability: Classic low-effort amusement park with carousels, a Ferris wheel, paddle boats on the pond, and a planetarium — ideal wind-down activity for both a 5- and 8-year-old after a museum visit; minimal walking, plenty of benches/shade, stroller-friendly paths.
- Source: [Gorky Park (Minsk) – Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gorky_Park_(Minsk)), [Gorky Central Park – Tripadvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g294448-d4283941-Reviews-Gorky_Central_Park-Minsk.html)

*(Lunch/dinner windows around items 2 and 3 are food-agent's responsibility — not duplicated here.)*

**Day 1 has both a culture item (National History Museum) and a park/nature item (Gorky Park), plus a free bonus walk — meets the "kid-friendly item each day" and interest-coverage requirements on Day 1.**

### Day 2 — 2026-07-05 (usable ~08:00–17:20, before 18:11 return train)

**4. Central Botanical Garden (Цэнтральны батанічны сад)**
- Type: Outdoor/park & nature — **second park/nature item, low-cost**
- Duration: ~1.5–2 hours
- Cost: Sourced winter/spring pricing was **7.50–14.50 BYN per ticket** (period-specific, e.g. Nov 2025/Apr 2025 dates). **Assumption (explicit, unchanged from v1): summer (July) pricing and hours were not directly confirmed by search** — assume similar or slightly higher summer-season pricing. Children **under 7 enter free** (confirmed), so the 5-year-old is free. **v2 planning figure (per iteration-plan-v1 guidance): adopt the LOW END → 3 paying tickets (2 adults + 8-year-old) × ~7.50 BYN ≈ 22 BYN total for the family** (v1 had used a ~22–30 BYN band; the recommended planning figure is now fixed at 22 BYN, not the high end), pending on-site/summer confirmation. Children under 13 must be accompanied by an adult (already the case here).
- Location/access: Metro to Akademiya Navuk station (M1 line), short walk to the main entrance.
- Family suitability: Wide, mostly flat paved paths through greenery, ponds, and flower displays — good low-exertion nature activity for a 5-year-old (room to run, benches to rest) and visually engaging for an 8-year-old (labeled plant collections). Stroller-friendly. Recommend the section nearest the main entrance to limit walking distance, given the reserve's large total size.
- Source: [cbg.org.by – Stoimost (pricing)](https://cbg.org.by/informatsiya/stoimost.php), [Botanical Garden Minsk – tropinki.by](https://tropinki.by/minsk/botanicheskij-sad-v-minske)

**5. Free wind-down: Ploshcha Nezalezhnasci (Independence Square) & Upper Town shops — RECOMMENDED core Day 2 second activity**
- Type: Culture (low-key, free) — **counts as Day 2's culture/museum-adjacent item in the core recommended plan**
- Duration: ~45 min–1 hour
- Cost: 0 BYN
- Location/access: Central, near Ploshcha Lyenina/Ploshcha Nezalezhnasci metro, on the way back toward the station.
- Family suitability: No ticket, no queue, minimal walking — a gentle close to Day 2 before the 17:40 station cutoff; easy pace for a 5-year-old, still has street performers/architecture/window shopping to hold an 8-year-old's interest.
- Source: [Free Attractions in Minsk 2026](https://allinmap.app/en/city-guides/free-attractions-minsk)

**Day 2 has both a park/nature item (Botanical Garden) and a free culture item (Independence Square/Upper Town shops) — meets the "kid-friendly item each day" and interest-coverage requirements on Day 2, at 0 additional BYN beyond the Garden.**
