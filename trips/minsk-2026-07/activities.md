# Activities — Minsk, Belarus (2026-07-04 to 2026-07-05)

Family: 2 adults + 2 children (ages 5 and 8), budget tier.
Main interests: Museums & culture; Parks & nature.
Sightseeing windows (per `route.md`): **Day 1 ≈ 12:15–20:00** (~7.5h, after 11:54 train arrival), **Day 2 ≈ 08:00–17:20** (~9h, before 18:11 return-train cutoff).

Base of operations: Minsk-Pasazhirsky train station sits directly at **Ploshcha Lyenina** metro station (Maskoŭskaja / M1 line), putting the whole city center within a short walk/one-metro-hop — source: [Minsk railway station – Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minsk_railway_station), [Ploshcha Lyenina (Minsk Metro) – Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ploshcha_Lyenina_(Minsk_Metro)). Single metro ride ≈ **0.90–1.15 BYN/person** (fares vary by source/date) — source: [minsknotdead.com – Minsk metro basics](https://minsknotdead.com/post/minsk-metro). In-city routing/fares in detail are transport-agent's remit; only walking/metro hints are given here for feasibility.

## Activities by Stop/Day

### Day 1 — 2026-07-04 (arrival 11:54, usable ~12:15–20:00)

**1. Upper Town & Trinity Hill (Verkhni Horad / Troitskoye Predmestye) walk**
- Type: Culture (free open-air historic quarter)
- Duration: ~1.5 hours
- Cost: **0 BYN** (free to walk; optional cafe stop is a food-agent item)
- Location/access: ~15–20 min walk from Ploshcha Lyenina metro (station exit), or one stop to Kastrychnickaya/Kupalauskaya then a short walk. Compact, flat, pedestrian-only cobblestone streets.
- Family suitability: Short, flat, stroller/kid-pace walk past colorful restored 19th-century buildings, the Holy Spirit Cathedral exterior, and the riverside — easy for a 5-year-old, visually interesting for an 8-year-old, no ticket/queue stress. No indoor accessibility issue since it's outdoor and level.
- Source: [In Your Pocket – Troitskoye Predmestye](https://www.inyourpocket.com/minsk/Troitskoye-Predmestye-Trinity-Suburb_2472v), [Free Attractions in Minsk 2026](https://allinmap.app/en/city-guides/free-attractions-minsk)

**2. National History Museum of the Republic of Belarus**
- Type: Culture (museum) — **satisfies the museum/culture interest requirement**
- Duration: ~1–1.5 hours (curated, kid-paced visit rather than full collection)
- Cost: Open daily 11:00–19:00, at 12 Karl Marx St, ~5–10 min walk from Upper Town. **Assumption (explicit): exact 2026 ticket price not confirmed by search** (museum's own price list page did not surface a figure); using a typical Belarusian state-museum price band as a placeholder: **adult ≈ 8 BYN × 2 = 16 BYN, child (8yo) ≈ 4 BYN, child (5yo, likely free under a young-child threshold) ≈ 0 BYN → family estimate ≈ 20 BYN**. Downstream budget-agent should treat this as a rough placeholder pending confirmation at histmuseum.by.
- Location/access: 12 Karl Marx St, central Minsk; walkable from Upper Town or one metro stop from Ploshcha Lyenina/Kastrychnickaya.
- Family suitability: Central, short-visit-friendly museum; recommend targeting 1–2 kid-interesting halls (e.g., ancient/medieval artifacts, WWII-era section) rather than the full 500,000-item collection, to avoid museum fatigue for a 5-year-old. Indoor, air-conditioned, no major accessibility barriers noted.
- Source: [National History Museum of the Republic of Belarus – official (EN)](https://history.museum.by/en), [Belarusian National History Museum – Belarus.travel](https://en.belarus.travel/landmarks/belarusian-national-history-museum)

**3. Gorky Park (Minsk Central Children's Park named after Gorky)**
- Type: Outdoor/park + amusement rides — **satisfies the park/nature interest requirement**
- Duration: ~2 hours
- Cost: Park entry itself is free; pay-per-ride. Individual rides ≈ **1–5 BYN each**; Ferris wheel ≈ 15–20 BYN per cabin (cabin, not per person — pricing varies by source and should be re-verified locally). **Assumption: budget ~3–4 rides shared across the family ≈ 15–20 BYN total**, skipping the Ferris wheel if tight on budget.
- Location/access: Near Nyamiha metro station (M1 line) or a ~20-min walk from Upper Town along the river.
- Family suitability: Classic low-effort amusement park with carousels, a Ferris wheel, paddle boats on the pond, and a planetarium — ideal wind-down activity for both a 5- and 8-year-old after a museum visit; minimal walking, plenty of benches/shade, stroller-friendly paths.
- Source: [Gorky Park (Minsk) – Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gorky_Park_(Minsk)), [Gorky Central Park – Tripadvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g294448-d4283941-Reviews-Gorky_Central_Park-Minsk.html)

*(Lunch/dinner windows around items 2 and 3 are food-agent's responsibility — not duplicated here.)*

### Day 2 — 2026-07-05 (usable ~08:00–17:20, before 18:11 return train)

**4. Central Botanical Garden (Цэнтральны батанічны сад)**
- Type: Outdoor/park & nature — **second park/nature item, low-cost**
- Duration: ~1.5–2 hours
- Cost: Sourced winter/spring pricing was **7.50–14.50 BYN per ticket** (period-specific, e.g. Nov 2025/Apr 2025 dates). **Assumption (explicit): summer (July) pricing and hours were not directly confirmed by search** — assume similar or slightly higher summer-season pricing (denser bloom, longer opening hours) and a similar per-ticket band. Children **under 7 enter free** (confirmed), so the 5-year-old is free; assume the 8-year-old and 2 adults pay standard rate → **family estimate ≈ 3 paying tickets × ~7.50–10 BYN ≈ 22–30 BYN**, pending on-site/summer confirmation. Children under 13 must be accompanied by an adult (already the case here).
- Location/access: Metro to Akademiya Navuk station (M1 line), short walk to the main entrance.
- Family suitability: Wide, mostly flat paved paths through greenery, ponds, and flower displays — good low-exertion nature activity for a 5-year-old (room to run, benches to rest) and visually engaging for an 8-year-old (labeled plant collections). Stroller-friendly. Recommend the section nearest the main entrance to limit walking distance, given the reserve's large total size.
- Source: [cbg.org.by – Stoimost (pricing)](https://cbg.org.by/informatsiya/stoimost.php), [Botanical Garden Minsk – tropinki.by](https://tropinki.by/minsk/botanicheskij-sad-v-minske)

**5. Museum of Interactive Science "Kvantum" — *optional/budget-permitting add-on***
- Type: Culture/science museum (interactive, hands-on) — a stronger fit than a static museum for young kids, but adds notable cost
- Duration: ~1–1.5 hours
- Cost (confirmed): Weekday tickets — **adult 22 BYN, child 20 BYN**; weekend — adult 24 BYN, child 22 BYN. Children under 3 free; children under 14 must be with an adult. **Family estimate (weekday, 2 adults + 2 children, 5-year-old charged at child rate since under-3 exemption doesn't apply): 22×2 + 20×2 = 84 BYN.**
- Location/access: pr. Gazety Pravda 29 (basement of the ZAGS building, near Titan mall) — north of the center; requires an M1→M2 metro transfer or a longer walk, roughly 20–30 min transit from the Botanical Garden. Open daily 10:00–21:00.
- Family suitability: 200+ hands-on exhibits (aerodynamics, magnetism, optics, a mirror maze, a dark room) are genuinely engaging for both a 5- and 8-year-old — more so than a traditional glass-case museum — but the transit time and the ~84 BYN family cost are significant against a 300–500 BYN whole-trip budget.
- **Recommendation:** Include only if budget-agent confirms headroom after transport/hotel/food are tallied; otherwise substitute with a second free walk (e.g., Ploshcha Nezalezhnasci / Independence Square exterior sights) before heading to the station.
- Source: [kvantumminsk.by – Cost](https://kvantumminsk.by/cost), [Museum of Interactive Science Kvantum – Tripadvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attraction_Review-g294448-d12033883-Reviews-Museum_of_Interactive_Science_Kvantum-Minsk.html)

**6. Free wind-down alternative (if Kvantum is skipped): Ploshcha Nezalezhnasci (Independence Square) & Upper Town shops**
- Type: Culture (low-key, free)
- Duration: ~45 min–1 hour
- Cost: 0 BYN
- Location/access: Central, near Ploshcha Lyenina/Ploshcha Nezalezhnasci metro, on the way back toward the station.
- Family suitability: No ticket, no queue, minimal walking — a gentle close to Day 2 before the 17:40 station cutoff; good buffer activity if the Botanical Garden or transit runs long.
- Source: [Free Attractions in Minsk 2026](https://allinmap.app/en/city-guides/free-attractions-minsk)

*Return-to-station timing note: whichever Day 2 afternoon option is chosen, the family should be back at/near Minsk-Pasazhirsky (Ploshcha Lyenina) by ~17:40–17:45 per `route.md`'s QG2 buffer for the 18:11 return train.*
