## Against Limit

- User's stated budget ceiling (`requirements.md`): **~300–500 BYN total for the whole group** (transport + hotel + food + activities — this definition does **not** mention a contingency line).
- **Pre-contingency subtotal (460 BYN): UNDER the 500 BYN ceiling by 40 BYN.** This is the figure that maps directly to the ceiling as defined in `requirements.md` (it sums exactly the four named categories: transport, hotel, food, activities).
- **Total with the 10% contingency (506 BYN): OVER the 500 BYN ceiling by 6 BYN.** The contingency is a budget-agent assumption/safety buffer, not a category the user's ceiling definition includes.
- **Binding figure:** Per the ceiling's own definition (transport + hotel + food + activities only), the **460 BYN pre-contingency subtotal is the binding figure**, and it is **UNDER** the 500 BYN limit. The 506 BYN figure is a recommended planning cushion, not itself a ceiling breach — but it does mean there is effectively **no spare room** once a reasonable contingency is held, so any of the optional upgrades below (or a local-transport spend at the high end of its range) would likely push the trip over 500 BYN even counting only the core four categories.
