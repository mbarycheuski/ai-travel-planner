## Local Food to Try

- **Draniki** — grated potato pancakes, usually served with sour cream,
  sometimes with meat or mushroom filling; the single most iconic Belarusian
  dish. Available at Lido's cafeteria line (self-service Belarusian dishes
  are typically on offer) as well as at Vasilki, so the recommended
  cost-conscious plan does not require the higher-priced sit-down venue to
  sample this dish.
- **Machanka** — a rich pork-and-flour stew traditionally eaten with draniki
  or bread; hearty comfort food, good for kids who like stew/gravy dishes.
  Look for it on the Lido cafeteria line as a lower-cost way to try it.
- **Khaladnik (cold beet soup)** — a refreshing summer dish (relevant for a
  July trip), pink cold soup with beets, cucumber, egg, and dill; worth a
  small taste even for kids who are soup-averse (can be skipped if unwilling).
- **Kalduny** — small dumplings (similar to pelmeni/vareniki) filled with
  meat or mushrooms, usually a safe, kid-friendly bet.
- **Vareniki with berries** — sweet dumplings with cherry or berry filling,
  a good kid-friendly dessert option, commonly available at self-service
  Belarusian cafeterias as well as sit-down restaurants.
- Source for dish descriptions: [Vasilki menu – RestaurantGuru](https://restaurantguru.com/Vasilki-Minsk-15), [Where to Eat Well and Affordably in Minsk – BelaTravel](https://belatravel.by/en/where-to-eat-well-and-affordably-in-minsk/)
