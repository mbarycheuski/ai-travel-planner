## Rationale

- **Round-trip shape honored:** Matches requirements' explicit "Brest → Minsk → Brest" loop with a single intermediate city and one overnight stay — no detours added.
- **Family-friendly pacing over speed:** For both legs, a slightly slower/later train was chosen over the fastest available option specifically to avoid a pre-6 AM wake-up (Day 1) and a post-11 PM homecoming (Day 2) for a 5-year-old and 8-year-old — trading ~1–1.5 hours of extra sightseeing time for a more humane schedule, consistent with the requirements' family-friendly focus.
- **Maximizes usable Minsk time within that constraint:** Within the family-friendly band, the earliest reasonable outbound (08:09) and latest reasonable return (18:11) were chosen to preserve close to a full two days of activity time (~16.5–17 hours total), which activities-agent and food-agent should treat as the hard ceiling per QG2.
- **Central station location simplifies Day-1/Day-2 logistics:** Because Minsk-Pasazhirsky sits at Ploshcha Lyenina metro (city center), no extra "transfer to city" time needs to be budgeted beyond a short metro/walk hop — this keeps the sightseeing-hour estimates above realistic rather than optimistic.
- **No max daily travel-time cap was stated in requirements** beyond "assume the round-trip train legs are acceptable" (requirements.md assumptions) — both legs (~3h45m–3h53m) comfortably fit that assumption and are within a normal single intercity train ride for children of these ages.
