## Local Food to Try

- **Draniki** — grated potato pancakes, usually served with sour cream,
  sometimes with meat or mushroom filling; the single most iconic Belarusian
  dish, available at Vasilki and most Belarusian cafeterias/restaurants.
- **Machanka** — a rich pork-and-flour stew traditionally eaten with draniki
  or bread; hearty comfort food, good for kids who like stew/gravy dishes.
- **Khaladnik (cold beet soup)** — a refreshing summer dish (relevant for a
  July trip), pink cold soup with beets, cucumber, egg, and dill; worth a
  small taste even for kids who are soup-averse (can be skipped if unwilling).
- **Kalduny** — small dumplings (similar to pelmeni/vareniki) filled with
  meat or mushrooms, usually a safe, kid-friendly bet.
- **Draniki-based desserts / vareniki with berries** — sweet dumplings with
  cherry or berry filling, a good kid-friendly dessert option.
- Source for dish descriptions: [Vasilki menu – RestaurantGuru](https://restaurantguru.com/Vasilki-Minsk-15), [Where to Eat Well and Affordably in Minsk – BelaTravel](https://belatravel.by/en/where-to-eat-well-and-affordably-in-minsk/)

- **Cafeteria-style venues prioritized (Lido) for both "fast turnaround"
  meals** (Day 1 lunch right after train arrival, Day 2 lunch right before
  the hard 17:40 station cutoff) because self-service format minimizes wait
  time and lets each child pick their own food — directly serving the
  train-window constraint (QG2) and the young-children suitability
  requirement (QG3 equivalent for food/logistics, QG7).

  family gets a proper taste of Belarusian specialties (draniki, machanka)
  without time pressure, since Day 1 evening has no hard downstream
  deadline (per route.md's ~20:00 wind-down target, which is a soft
  pacing assumption, not a train constraint).
- **No dietary restrictions to accommodate** (per requirements.md
  assumption) — all recommended venues have varied, flexible menus, so no
  restaurant was excluded or specially selected for allergen/diet reasons;

- **All venues are central / metro-accessible from Minsk-Pasazhirsky
  (Ploshcha Lyenina) or Ploshcha Yakuba Kolasa**, consistent with the
