# Packing List — Minsk, Belarus (2026-07-04 to 2026-07-05)

**Trip:** Brest → Minsk → Brest, 2 days / 1 night, family of 4 (2 adults + children ages 5 and 8), intercity train (~4h each way), interests in museums/culture and parks/nature.

**Climate assumption:** Early July in Minsk is mild summer — average high ~23.5°C (74°F), average low ~13.3°C (56°F), ~36% chance of rain on any given day, humidity ~74% ([weatherspark.com](https://weatherspark.com/m/95128/7/Average-Weather-in-July-in-Minsk-Belarus)). Pack for warm days, cool evenings, and a realistic chance of showers — no heavy rain gear needed, but a light rain layer is worth carrying.

This is a short, 1-night trip — the guiding principle throughout is **carry-on only, light packing** (one shared family bag or backpack + one small day bag), since the family will be walking/using metro and carrying bags during museum and park visits.

---

## Clothing

- [ ] 2 sets of clothes per person (1 worn, 1 spare) — short-sleeve tops + light trousers/shorts
- [ ] 1 light long-sleeve layer per person (cool evenings, ~13°C low)
- [ ] 1 packable light rain jacket or poncho per person (36% rain chance — assumption: pack for possible showers, not guaranteed) ([weatherspark.com](https://weatherspark.com/m/95128/7/Average-Weather-in-July-in-Minsk-Belarus))
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes for everyone (closed-toe, already broken in — important given museum + park walking on both days)
- [ ] 1 spare pair of socks/underwear per person beyond what's worn
- [ ] Light hat/cap for sun (both kids + adults)
- [ ] Pajamas/sleepwear (1 set each, for the 1 night)
- [ ] Swim gear — optional, only if a park visit includes a splash pad/fountain area for the kids

## Electronics

- [ ] Phone chargers (1–2, shared)
- [ ] Portable power bank (useful for train ride + full sightseeing days)
- [ ] Headphones/earbuds for kids (train ride entertainment, keeps volume considerate of other passengers)
- [ ] Camera or phone for photos (museums/parks)
- [ ] Tablet pre-loaded with offline shows/games for train ride (optional but recommended for a 4h journey with a 5- and 8-year-old)

## Travel Documents

- [ ] ID/passports for all 4 travelers (adult IDs + children's IDs/birth certificates as required for domestic Belarusian travel)
- [ ] Train tickets — Brest→Minsk and Minsk→Brest (printed or downloaded, per transport-agent's confirmed times)
- [ ] Hotel/apartment booking confirmation (per accommodation-agent)
- [ ] Cash in BYN (small bills, budget tier — some vendors/parks may not take cards) + 1 card as backup
- [ ] Small day bag/backpack to carry documents + essentials during sightseeing (keep passports/tickets on the accompanying adult, not in checked luggage)

## Medicines & Health

- [ ] Any personal/prescription medication for each family member, in original packaging
- [ ] Basic first-aid kit: plasters/band-aids, antiseptic wipes, blister plasters (for the walking-heavy days)
- [ ] Children's pain/fever reliever (age-appropriate dosing) in case of fever or discomfort
- [ ] Motion sickness remedy if any family member is prone to it (train ride)
- [ ] Hand sanitizer / wet wipes (useful for kids during museum/park outings and the train)
- [ ] Insect repellent — light, for park/nature visits in summer

## Destination-Specific

- [ ] Sunscreen (kid-safe SPF for both children, adult SPF) — daytime UV exposure during park visits
- [ ] Reusable water bottles (1 per person) — refill for the train ride and while out at museums/parks, humidity ~74% makes hydration important ([weatherspark.com](https://weatherspark.com/m/95128/7/Average-Weather-in-July-in-Minsk-Belarus))
- [ ] Light snacks for the ~4h train ride each way (fruit, crackers, kid-friendly snacks — minimizes reliance on train catering)
- [ ] Small entertainment kit for kids on the train: coloring book/crayons, a couple of small toys, a simple travel game (age 5 and 8 appropriate)
- [ ] Comfort item(s) for the 5-year-old (small stuffed toy/blanket) for the train ride and overnight stay
- [ ] Reusable tote or foldable bag for museum brochures/souvenirs so the main bag isn't overloaded
- [ ] Small packet of tissues (museums/parks often lack supplies)
- [ ] Umbrella (compact, 1 shared) as a lightweight backup to the rain jackets, given the 36% rain chance ([weatherspark.com](https://weatherspark.com/m/95128/7/Average-Weather-in-July-in-Minsk-Belarus))

## Notes & Assumptions

- **Season/weather source:** Minsk early-July climate averages from Weather Spark ([weatherspark.com](https://weatherspark.com/m/95128/7/Average-Weather-in-July-in-Minsk-Belarus)) — used to justify the light-layers + light-rain-gear approach rather than heavy winter or full wet-weather gear.
- **Assumption:** No specific medical conditions or dietary restrictions noted in requirements.md for the family; medicine list is generic/precautionary.
- **Assumption:** Domestic travel within Belarus (Brest to Minsk) — document requirements kept general (ID/passport); no international entry requirements apply per requirements.md (destination and origin both in Belarus).
- Packing is deliberately minimal given the 1-night duration — prioritize a single shared bag plus one day bag to keep the family mobile on foot and via metro, consistent with the budget/transport assumptions in requirements.md.
