# Packing

## Weather Outlook

**Source/method:** Open-Meteo 7-day weather forecast (accessed 2026-07-10, valid 2026-07-12 to 2026-07-14), as recorded in `weather.md`. Single stop: Zakopane (Tatra region, ~819 m elevation) for the whole 2-night trip.

- **Sun 12 Jul — arrival day:** 11–15 °C, light rain nearly all day (97% precipitation probability). Cool and damp — waterproof outer layers essential, activities biased indoors (Tatra Museum, Aqua Park).
- **Mon 13 Jul — best weather day:** 12–20 °C, light drizzle easing through the morning, warming afternoon; driest of the three days. Good for the day's outdoor activities (Gubałówka funicular, Dolina Strążyska valley walk, Wielka Krokiew ski-jump viewing), but a damp morning still calls for a light waterproof layer.
- **Tue 14 Jul — departure day, warmest:** 12–21 °C, light intermittent drizzle (94% probability). Manageable but still rain-prone — pack the waterproof jacket for the morning park/playground outing before the drive home.

**Overall implication:** Every day sits in the cool 11–21 °C band with meaningful rain probability — pack for layering plus reliable rain protection rather than summer heat, and bring swimwear for the hotel's indoor pool/sauna and the Aqua Park regardless of outdoor conditions.

## Clothing

### Adults (x2)

- [ ] Waterproof/rain jacket (packable, hooded) — 1 per adult
- [ ] Fleece or warm mid-layer (evenings dip toward 11–12 °C)
- [ ] 3–4x long-sleeve tops / t-shirts for layering
- [ ] 1–2x light trousers/jeans (quick-drying preferred)
- [ ] Sturdy closed-toe walking shoes (for Dolina Strążyska valley trail and Gubałówka hilltop paths)
- [ ] Comfortable town shoes (for Krupówki stroll, museum)
- [ ] Swimwear (Hotel Helios indoor pool/sauna + Aqua Park Zakopane)
- [ ] Warm socks (2–3 pairs) + spare socks for wet feet
- [ ] Sleepwear
- [ ] Underwear (enough for 3 days, or fewer with hotel laundry not assumed)
- [ ] Light hat or cap (sun breaks possible afternoons) + warm beanie for cool mornings/evenings

### Children (age 5 and age 8)

- [ ] Waterproof jacket with hood — 1 per child (bright colour recommended for visibility on trails)
- [ ] Waterproof trousers or rain-suit (useful for the wetter Day 1 and damp Day 3 morning outings)
- [ ] Fleece/warm layer for both children
- [ ] 3–4x t-shirts/long-sleeve tops each
- [ ] Sturdy waterproof or water-resistant walking shoes/boots (Dolina Strążyska path is gravel/stream-side, can be muddy after rain)
- [ ] Wellington boots or spare shoes for wet play (Park Miejski playground, Gubałówka hilltop grounds)
- [ ] Swimwear + swim nappies if needed (Aqua Park's shallow children's pools, hotel pool)
- [ ] Extra full change of clothes each (kept in day bag — useful after pool visits or muddy walks)
- [ ] Warm socks and spare socks (2–3 pairs each)
- [ ] Sleepwear
- [ ] Underwear (enough for 3 days)
- [ ] Warm hat/beanie for cool mornings and the Gubałówka hilltop (windier, higher elevation)
- [ ] Comfort item(s) for the ~5-hour car journeys each way (favourite toy/blanket)

### Shared/Family

- [ ] Umbrella (compact, 1–2 for the family) — backup for the museum/Krupówki stroll where a jacket alone may not be enough
- [ ] Reusable swim bag / wet-clothes bag (for Aqua Park and hotel pool sessions)
- [ ] Quick-dry travel towel(s) if not provided at Aqua Park (confirm on arrival; hotel pool towels likely provided)

## Electronics

- [ ] Phone chargers (1 per adult) + car charger/adapter for the drive
- [ ] Power bank (useful during the ~5-hour drives and full activity days)
- [ ] Camera (optional — for Tatra views from Gubałówka/Wielka Krokiew)
- [ ] Tablet/handheld device + headphones for the children (long car legs, per `transport.md`'s ~1h50m + ~3h05m driving segments each way)
- [ ] Car phone mount/navigation setup (own car trip, per `transport.md`)
- [ ] EU-standard plug devices only needed (Poland uses Type C/E, 230V — no adapter required for Polish/EU-bought devices)

## Travel Documents

- [ ] National ID cards (dowód osobisty) or passports for all 4 family members — domestic Poland trip (Łódź → Zakopane), no border crossing, but ID should still be carried per standard Polish practice.
- [ ] Driver's licence (valid, photo ID)
- [ ] Vehicle registration papers (dowód rejestracyjny)
- [ ] Proof of vehicle insurance (OC policy documents)
- [ ] Printed/digital hotel booking confirmation — Hotel Helios (per `accommodation.md`)
- [ ] Printed/digital tickets or booking references for Aqua Park, Gubałówka funicular, Tatra Museum, Wielka Krokiew, Mini Club (per `activities.md`)
- [ ] Cash (PLN) and/or card for parking fees, Tatra National Park entrance, and smaller vendors that may not accept cards (per `transport.md`)
- [ ] Car equipment required by Polish law: warning triangle and fire extinguisher (check own car is fitted — legal requirement, not a "pack" item but verify before departure)

## Medicines & Health

- [ ] Basic first-aid kit (plasters/band-aids, antiseptic wipes, blister plasters — useful for the Dolina Strążyska walk)
- [ ] Any personal/prescription medication for all 4 family members, in original packaging
- [ ] Children's pain/fever reliever (age-appropriate doses for a 5- and 8-year-old)
- [ ] Motion sickness remedy for children if prone, for the ~5-hour car legs
- [ ] Rehydration sachets (useful after Aqua Park / active days)
- [ ] Insect repellent (light, for the valley walk)
- [ ] Basic sunscreen (SPF) despite the rain forecast — mountain UV can be strong when clouds break, especially on Days 2–3's warmer afternoons
- [ ] Hand sanitiser / wet wipes (especially useful with young children at pools, playgrounds, museum)
- [ ] Travel-sized tissues

## Destination-Specific

- [ ] Swimwear + goggles for kids (Aqua Park Zakopane's slides/shallow pools and Hotel Helios's indoor pool/sauna, per `accommodation.md` and `activities.md`)
- [ ] Flip-flops/pool sandals for hotel pool, sauna, and Aqua Park changing areas
- [ ] Sturdy walking shoes (adults and children) for Dolina Strążyska's gravel/stream-side trail — can be muddy/slippery given the forecast rain
- [ ] Layered clothing for temperature swings between valley towns and the higher, windier Gubałówka hilltop and Wielka Krokiew viewing platform
- [ ] Small daypack/backpack to carry rain gear, water, and snacks on the Day 2 outdoor circuit (Gubałówka + Dolina Strążyska + Wielka Krokiew)
- [ ] Reusable water bottles for the family (refill before valley walk and hilltop outings)
- [ ] Small stroller or child-carrier if the 5-year-old tires easily — Gubałówka funicular and Krupówki are stroller-friendly; Dolina Strążyska's final stretch to the waterfall is narrower and walked on foot
- [ ] Snacks for the car journeys and between-activity stretches (per the relaxed, break-friendly pace noted in `activities.md` and the Częstochowa rest stop in `transport.md`)
- [ ] Car essentials per Polish law: warning triangle, fire extinguisher, reflective vest(s) — confirm presence in own car before departure
