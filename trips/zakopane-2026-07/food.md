## Estimated Food Cost

Assumptions: 3 calendar days (2026-07-12, -13, -14), family of 4 (2 adults + 2 children). Children's portions/meals assumed at roughly 60% of an adult's cost (smaller portions, milk-bar/kids-menu pricing), consistent with the budget-conscious milk-bar/highlander-bistro picks above rather than fine dining.

**Per-day cost model (family of 4 — 2 adults + 2 children):**

- Breakfast: mostly self-catered/hotel or a light bakery item — assumed **20 PLN/adult, 10 PLN/child** = (20×2)+(10×2) = **60 PLN/day**
- Lunch (milk bar / rest-stop cafe): **35 PLN/adult, 20 PLN/child** = (35×2)+(20×2) = **110 PLN/day**
- Dinner (highlander bistro, sit-down): **45 PLN/adult, 27 PLN/child** = (45×2)+(27×2) = **144 PLN/day**
- Daily total: 60 + 110 + 144 = **314 PLN/day**

**3-day total:** 314 PLN × 3 days = **942 PLN**

Additional one-off treats (oscypek snack from a stall, ice cream) budgeted separately: assume ~15 PLN/person once during the trip = 15 × 4 = **60 PLN**.

**Estimated total food cost for the trip (family of 4): 942 + 60 = ≈ 1,000 PLN**

This fits within the ~1,550–2,550 PLN remaining after transport (per `transport.md`), leaving headroom for accommodation and activities within the overall 2,000–3,000 PLN trip budget (QG1).

## Rationale & Assumptions

- **No dietary restrictions to accommodate** (per `requirements-v2.md`), so the "Dietary Fit" column above simply confirms no conflicts rather than flagging vegetarian/allergen alternatives; all picks are standard Polish/highlander fare.
- **Kid-suitability (QG7) prioritized over fine dining**: every restaurant chosen is casual/family-style (milk bars, bistros, a restaurant with an explicit kids' corner) — no adults-only bars, tasting menus, or venues requiring quiet/formal behavior from a 5- and 8-year-old.
- **Milk bars (bary mleczne) chosen deliberately** for Zakopane as the most budget-conscious, authentically local option — cheap, fast, large portions, and traditionally very tolerant of families and children (self-service canteen style, no long wait for service).
- **Częstochowa lunch stop** is included because `transport.md`'s Legs table designates it as a rest/lunch stop on both driving days (2026-07-12 and 2026-07-14), even though it is not listed as an overnight stop in the Stops & Nights table; a single family-friendly option is provided to cover both passes through, consistent with covering the itinerary the family will actually follow.
- **Łódź departure-day breakfast** is included as a practical real-world need (the family needs to eat before a multi-hour drive) even though Łódź has 0 nights in the Stops & Nights table; only one quick, verified option is given since it's not a core "stop."
- **Prices for Krupowa Izba and the general Częstochowa restaurant range** were not published on their own sites; the ranges given are estimated from comparable, verified nearby establishments' pricing (Schronisko Krupówki, zakopane.info roundup) rather than invented figures — flagged here explicitly as an estimate, not a sourced menu price.
- **Child-portion cost ratio (60% of adult)** in the cost model is an assumption, not sourced from any single restaurant's kids'-menu pricing, since none of the verified sources published a separate children's price; it is a reasonable planning estimate for a budget-conscious family trip.
- **One meal per day per venue type** (breakfast/lunch/dinner) is assumed as the planning unit; actual spend will vary if the family skips a meal out (e.g. self-catering breakfast at the hotel) or adds extra snacks/ice cream beyond the 60 PLN one-off treat allowance.
- **TripAdvisor deliberately avoided** as a citation source per prior feedback; all links above are to official restaurant sites, restaurant-review aggregators (Wanderlog), or informational travel-guide sites (zakopane.info, KrakowBuzz) that were directly fetched and verified to load and support the claims made.
