## Rationale & Assumptions

- **Heat/UV scheduling (weather.md)**: every outdoor item (Belém Tower, Jerónimos cloisters, Praça do Comércio/Arch, São Jorge Castle) is scheduled before ~11am or after ~5pm, matching weather.md's "schedule sightseeing for early morning or late afternoon/evening" guidance for the very-high/extreme UV (7.65–7.85) and 30–34°C highs forecast Aug 1–5. The 12pm–4pm midday window is filled with air-conditioned indoor venues (Centro Colombo, Oceanário de Lisboa, Pavilhão do Conhecimento) on city days, or the day trip to the noticeably cooler, breeze-moderated Cascais coast (21–28°C per weather.md) on Day 4 — flipping the "indoor midday" rule for that one day because the coast itself is the heat relief.
- **Minimal-walking constraint**: Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery are adjacent (short taxi/tram-15 hop, not a walking tour); Oceanário, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, and the Telecabine cable car all sit within the same flat Parque das Nações riverside precinct; São Jorge Castle is reached by taxi/tuk-tuk to minimize the hill climb; the Cascais beach is a 5-minute flat walk from the train station. No day requires an extended walking tour between dispersed sights.
- **No duplicates**: eight distinct attractions are used across the 5 days (Rua Augusta Arch, Belém Tower, Jerónimos Monastery, Centro Colombo, Oceanário de Lisboa, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, Telecabine Lisboa, São Jorge Castle) plus the Cascais beach/old town — none repeated.
- **Child suitability**: every activity includes a family/accessibility note; children's rates or free-under-age-thresholds were applied per each venue's published policy (verified per source) — Belém Tower charges from age 6 (the 8-year-old pays, the 5-year-old is free), while Jerónimos Monastery and São Jorge Castle are free under 12 for both children.
- **Rua Augusta Arch children's pricing**: the official page did not state a child rate; consistent with nearly every other Lisbon monument researched (free under-12), children are assumed free at the arch. Flagged for `budget-aggregator`/`validator` as a minor assumption, not a confirmed published rate.
- **Currency**: all costs are EUR-native from each official/review source (oceanario.pt, torrebelem.com aggregator, lisbon.net, pavconhecimento.pt, telecabinelisboa.pt, visitlisboa.com, lisbonbeachesguide.com, cascaisportugaltourism.com) — no currency conversion was needed, matching the EUR trip currency in requirements-v2.md.
- **Cascais/Estoril day trip scope**: per transport.md's CP suburban train option (Cais do Sodré ↔ Cascais, ~33–40 min each way, already budgeted there), Day 4 uses Cascais specifically (Praia da Ribeira, calm/shallow, explicitly recommended for young children) rather than Estoril, since research found Cascais' town beach better suited to ages 5 and 8; Estoril remains a viable swap if the family prefers on the day, but is not separately costed to avoid double-booking a beach day.
- **Telecabine Lisboa seasonal hours**: the fetched schedule reflects a 2025 seasonal calendar (Aug 2026 not yet published); the "10:30am–8pm" high-season window is assumed to apply for early August 2026 based on the consistent yearly pattern — reconfirm closer to travel date.
- **Discretionary/optional spend** (Centro Colombo arcade ~€15, Cascais beach rental ~€10) are planning estimates for optional extras, not fixed admission fees, and are included in the total for budget-aggregator completeness.
- **Pool/downtime**: no separate line item is included for hotel-pool relaxation during the heat window on Days 2–3, since the pool itself is costed under `accommodation.md`; it is assumed the family also uses hotel pool time as unstructured heat-window downtime alongside the indoor activities listed.
- **Meaningful activity per day**: every day (including the arrival and departure days) carries at least one scheduled, linked activity, per the execution-plan.md requirement.
