🗓️ Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1 — Saturday, Aug 1, 2026 — Lisbon (Arrival)

  • TransportWizz Air, Warsaw Chopin (WAW) → Lisbon (LIS), nonstop, ~3h50m–4h20m. Then taxi/Bolt/Uber, Airport → Hotel, ~15–20 min, door-to-door.
  • EveningPraça do Comércio & Rua Augusta Arch — riverside square and viewing terrace (elevator to the top, no stairs), ~1 hr, scheduled early evening (~6–7pm) once the heat/UV has dropped.
  • DiningCasual arrival-evening meal at Bonjardim ("Rei dos Frangos") — piri-piri grilled chicken, fast turnaround, good for tired children after travel.
  • OvernightVIP Executive Éden Aparthotel, 4-star, Restauradores Square, Baixa/Chiado.

Day 2 — Sunday, Aug 2, 2026 — Lisbon

  • 09:30Belém Tower, ~9:30–10:30am (before peak heat/UV); ground floor flat/buggy-accessible, upper battlements optional (stairs).
  • 10:45Jerónimos Monastery, ~10:45–11:45am, shaded stone cloisters, short taxi/tram hop from Belém.
  • MiddayCentro Colombo shopping mall, ~12pm–4pm heat window — air-conditioned, kids' play area, arcade, cinema, stroller-friendly.
  • DiningPáteo – Bairro do Avillez (Chiado) — contemporary Portuguese, kids' portions, on-site ice-cream cart.
  • OvernightVIP Executive Éden Aparthotel.

Day 3 — Monday, Aug 3, 2026 — Lisbon

  • 12:00Oceanário de Lisboa, 12:00–15:30 slot — air-conditioned, ramps/lifts, stroller-friendly, baby-changing facilities.
  • AfternoonPavilhão do Conhecimento (interactive science museum, same Parque das Nações precinct) — hands-on exhibits for ages 5–8.
  • 17:30Telecabine Lisboa cable car, ~5:30–6pm — seated ride, river breeze, gentle motion suitable for a 5-year-old.
  • DiningTime Out Market Lisboa (Cais do Sodré) — food-hall format, variety for varying kids' tastes, no fixed-menu wait.
  • OvernightVIP Executive Éden Aparthotel.

Day 4 — Tuesday, Aug 4, 2026 — Cascais Day Trip

  • TransportCP suburban train, Cais do Sodré ↔ Cascais, ~33–40 min each way.
  • MiddayPraia da Ribeira beach, Cascais, midday (~3–4 hrs) — calm, sheltered, shallow water "ideal for children"; no lifeguard on this beach, direct adult supervision needed.
  • 16:30Cascais old town & marina waterfront stroll, ~4:30–5:30pm, flat pedestrian waterfront, shaded arcades and cafés, before the return train.
  • DiningSnacks/light meal in Cascais old town (waterfront cafés) — no specific restaurant recommendation sourced for this stop beyond the old-town stroll note.
  • OvernightVIP Executive Éden Aparthotel, back in Lisbon.

Day 5 — Wednesday, Aug 5, 2026 — Lisbon (Departure)

  • 09:00São Jorge Castle (Castelo de São Jorge), ~9:00–10:30am (before peak heat/UV and well before the flight) — taxi/tuk-tuk drop-off near the entrance to minimize the hill climb; flat grounds within the ramparts; panoramic views.
  • MiddayLight lunch before departure — see general Lisbon dining picks (e.g. Stō Restaurante & Mercearia, near Arco da Rua Augusta) if time permits before the airport transfer.
  • TransportTaxi/Bolt/Uber, Hotel → Airport, ~15–20 min, then Wizz Air, Lisbon (LIS) → Warsaw Chopin (WAW), nonstop, ~3h50m–4h20m.

💡 Travel Tips

  • Heat and UV: Daily highs of 30–34°C and a "very high" to "extreme" UV index (7.65–8.2) are expected. Reapply SPF 50+ sunscreen every 2 hours, use hats and light long sleeves at midday, and favor early morning/late afternoon for outdoor sightseeing — the itinerary is already built around this.
  • Minimal-walking constraint: Taxi/Bolt/Uber is used in preference to the metro for most transfers (airport and in-city) given two young children and the heat; no day requires an extended walking tour between dispersed sights.
  • Dietary/accessibility: No dietary constraints or accessibility needs were stated for this trip; all dining and lodging picks reflect that (no allergen filtering applied, no step-free-access filtering applied).
  • Planning estimates to reconfirm at booking: the flight fare (~€230/person round trip) and the hotel rate (~€190/night) are both planning estimates pending date-specific reconfirmation — even the worst-case scenario still leaves an 18% budget buffer, but reconfirm both closer to booking.
  • Local-transport allowance: the €150–€200 local-transport estimate is a planning placeholder (midpoint €175 used in the budget total above); actual taxi/transit mix may vary slightly day to day.
  • Cascais beach supervision: Praia da Ribeira has no lifeguard on duty — direct adult supervision is needed, and the sea temperature (~18–19°C) is cool for children, so rash guards/wetsuit tops are recommended.
  • Adults-only note: Ginjinha (sour-cherry liqueur) tasting is a fun quick stop for the adults near Rossio Square but is not intended for the children.
