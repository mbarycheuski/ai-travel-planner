## Estimated Food Cost

Assumptions: 2 adults + 2 children (ages 5, 8) for 4 nights / 5 days in Lisbon (Aug 1 arrival through Aug 5 departure — treated as breakfast/lunch/dinner coverage across 4 full dining days, with lighter arrival/departure days). Children's meals estimated at roughly 60% of an adult portion cost (kids' menus/smaller portions typical in Lisbon). Breakfast is assumed included at the 4-star hotel (per `accommodation.md`) and is **not** counted here; this covers lunch, dinner, and snacks/pastries only.

**Per-day estimate (party of 4):**
- Lunch (casual — e.g. Bonjardim, Time Out Market, or a tasca): 2 adults × €15 + 2 children × €9 = €30 + €18 = **€48**
- Dinner (mix of casual and one "destination" dinner like Páteo or Cervejaria Ramiro, averaged): 2 adults × €35 + 2 children × €18 = €70 + €36 = **€106**
- Snacks/pastries (pastel de nata, water, ice cream, coffee for adults): **€12/day** for the party

**Per-day total: €48 + €106 + €12 = €166/day**

**Total across 4 full dining days (Aug 1 evening arrival through Aug 5 morning departure ≈ 4 dinners + 4 lunches + snacks, treated as 4 day-equivalents):**

4 × €166 = **€664**

**Estimated total food cost for the trip (party of 4): ~€664** (excludes hotel breakfast, which is already covered by `accommodation.md`).

## Rationale & Assumptions

- **Single stop covered**: `transport.md`'s `## Stops & Nights` lists exactly one stop — Lisbon, 4 nights — so all picks are concentrated there; no other stops require coverage.
- **Dietary constraints**: `requirements-v2.md` states "Dietary constraints: None." All five restaurant picks are marked with "No dietary restrictions stated" and no allergen/vegetarian/etc. flag was required; this will be revisited only if a later revision of requirements introduces a constraint.
- **Minimal-walking constraint**: per the hard pacing constraint in `requirements-v2.md`/`transport.md`, all five picks are either within a ~2–10 minute walk of the Baixa-Chiado accommodation area or explicitly flagged as needing a short taxi ride (Time Out Market at Cais do Sodré, ~10–12 min taxi; Cervejaria Ramiro on Av. Almirante Reis, ~10 min taxi). No pick requires a long walk; taxi/Bolt fares for these short hops are already budgeted under `transport.md`'s local-transport allowance (€150–€200), not double-counted here.
- **Child suitability (QG6)**: every restaurant was chosen specifically because sources confirm family/kid-friendliness — Páteo (ice-cream cart, spacious seating, staff who guide families), Bonjardim (fast, casual, universally liked grilled chicken), Stō (cozy tasca, kid-friendly traditional dishes), Time Out Market (food-hall format ideal for varying kids' tastes, communal seating, no fixed-menu wait), and Cervejaria Ramiro (casual/boisterous, tolerant of children, though flagged as best suited to an early dinner given its bustling atmosphere and per-kg seafood pricing).
- **Currency**: all prices are quoted directly in EUR from EUR-native sources (official restaurant/venue sites, RestaurantGuru, Eating Europe, Taste of Lisboa, Devour Tours) — trip currency per `requirements-v2.md`. No currency conversion was required.
- **Price-range sourcing**: where an official site did not publish exact menu prices (e.g. Manteigaria, Páteo's exact per-dish figures), price ranges are drawn from independently verified review/food-blog sources cross-checked against multiple search results (e.g. Cervejaria Ramiro's per-kg seafood pricing, confirmed at roughly €75–100 for two across several independent sources).
- **Estimated Food Cost methodology**: children's meal cost is assumed at ~60% of an adult's price point (kids' menus / smaller portions are standard in Lisbon restaurants, consistent with sources noting dedicated children's menus at family-oriented venues). Hotel breakfast is assumed included in the accommodation rate (per `accommodation.md`) and is therefore excluded from this food-cost total to avoid double-counting with `budget-aggregator`'s consolidated total. The €664 estimate is a planning figure for lunches, dinners, and snacks only, averaged across one "destination" dinner (Páteo or Ramiro) and several more casual meals (Bonjardim, Stō, Time Out Market) over the 4-night stay.
- **A Ginjinha** is listed under "Local Food to Try" as an adults-only cultural stop (traditional cherry liqueur bar, no seating, cash-only) — flagged explicitly as not intended for the two children, included only as a quick, low-cost detour for the adults while passing through Rossio.
