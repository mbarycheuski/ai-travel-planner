**Heat and UV implications for family with minimal-walking constraint:**
- August is peak summer heat in Lisbon. Daily highs of 30–34°C are typical and comfortable for short exposures, but sustained outdoor walking—especially with children aged 5 and 8—risks heat exhaustion and severe sunburn.
- UV index of 7.5–8.2 ("very high" to "extreme") requires strong sun protection: SPF 50+ sunscreen reapplied every 2 hours, hats, and lightweight long sleeves for children during midday.
- Schedule sightseeing for early morning (before 10 a.m.) or late afternoon/evening (after 5 p.m.) when UV is lower and temperatures drop 5–10°C.
- Plan air-conditioned indoor activities (museums, shopping centers, family restaurants, indoor pools at hotel) for 11 a.m.–5 p.m. peak heat window.
- Nights cool to ~18–21°C, comfortable for evening strolls or outdoor dining if shaded.

**Precipitation:** Virtually no rain expected across all five days. Pack no rain gear; focus packing on sun and heat protection.

## Cascais and Estoril Beach Areas

**Method:** Seasonal forecast ensemble (Open-Meteo seasonal model for Aug 1–5). Note: Cascais/Estoril are ~25 km west of Lisbon city center on the coast, with slightly cooler sea-influenced temperatures and brisk Atlantic breezes.

**Temperature and conditions:**
- **Aug 1–3:** Highs 21–28°C (notably cooler than Lisbon city). Lows 17–19°C.
- **Aug 4–5:** Highs 28–32°C. Lows 17–19°C. Sea breeze moderates extreme heat.

**Beach suitability:**
- **Sea temperature in early August:** ~18–19°C typical for Portuguese Atlantic coast. Wetsuits or rash guards recommended for children; many families find water refreshing but not warm. Shallow, protected beach areas (e.g., Cascais town beach) are most suitable for ages 5 and 8.
- **UV and sun:** Coastal areas have no shade relief from UV; reflective glare off sand and water intensifies burn risk. Strong sun protection essential even with sea breeze.
- **Wind and waves:** August typically sees moderate Atlantic swell (0.5–1.5 m); conditions generally safe for family swimming, but check daily beach reports and lifeguard status.
- **Heat advantage:** Cooler seaside temperatures and ocean breeze make beach days more tolerable than city sightseeing during 11 a.m.–5 p.m. heat window. Plan beach outings for midday, when urban Lisbon is hottest.
