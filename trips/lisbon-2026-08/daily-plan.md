# Lisbon Family Trip — August 1–5, 2026

## Trip Summary

- **Destination:** Lisbon, Portugal (single base for the whole stay), with a day trip to Cascais
- **Dates:** Sat, Aug 1 – Wed, Aug 5, 2026 (4 nights / 5 days)
- **Party:** 2 adults + 2 children (ages 5 and 8)
- **Transport mode:** Flight (Warsaw ⇄ Lisbon), with taxi/rideshare and public transit locally
- **Total estimated cost:** €2,846.50 (midpoint scenario) vs. €3,500 budget cap — **under by €653.50 (~18.7% buffer)**
- **Trip shape:** Relaxed, low-walking pace built around 2 young children and August heat — outdoor sights scheduled early morning/late afternoon, air-conditioned indoor activities during the midday heat window, one Cascais beach day, taxi-first local transport

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1 — Saturday, Aug 1, 2026 — Lisbon (Arrival)

- **Transport:** [Wizz Air, Warsaw Chopin (WAW) → Lisbon (LIS)](https://us.trip.com/flights/warsaw-to-lisbon/airfares-waw-lis/), nonstop, ~3h50m–4h20m. Then [taxi/Bolt/Uber, Airport → Hotel](https://www.worldtaximeter.com/lisbon/Lisbon+airport/baixa+chiado), ~15–20 min, door-to-door.
- **Activity:** [Praça do Comércio & Rua Augusta Arch](https://www.visitlisboa.com/en/places/arco-da-rua-augusta) — riverside square and viewing terrace (elevator to the top, no stairs), ~1 hr, scheduled early evening (~6–7pm) once the heat/UV has dropped.
- **Dining:** Casual arrival-evening meal at [Bonjardim ("Rei dos Frangos")](https://restaurantguru.com/Bonjardim-Lisbon) — piri-piri grilled chicken, fast turnaround, good for tired children after travel.
- **Overnight:** [VIP Executive Éden Aparthotel](https://www.viphotels.com/en/hotels/vip-executive-eden-in-lisbon/), 4-star, Restauradores Square, Baixa/Chiado.

### Day 2 — Sunday, Aug 2, 2026 — Lisbon

- **Activities:**
  - [Belém Tower](https://torrebelem.com/en/), ~9:30–10:30am (before peak heat/UV); ground floor flat/buggy-accessible, upper battlements optional (stairs).
  - [Jerónimos Monastery](https://www.lisbon.net/jeronimos-monastery), ~10:45–11:45am, shaded stone cloisters, short taxi/tram hop from Belém.
  - [Centro Colombo shopping mall](https://www.visitlisboa.com/en/places/colombo-shopping-center), ~12pm–4pm heat window — air-conditioned, kids' play area, arcade, cinema, stroller-friendly.
- **Dining:** [Páteo – Bairro do Avillez](https://bairrodoavillez.pt/en/pateo/) (Chiado) — contemporary Portuguese, kids' portions, on-site ice-cream cart.
- **Overnight:** [VIP Executive Éden Aparthotel](https://www.viphotels.com/en/hotels/vip-executive-eden-in-lisbon/).

### Day 3 — Monday, Aug 3, 2026 — Lisbon

- **Activities:**
  - [Oceanário de Lisboa](https://oceanario.pt/en/visit/tickets/), 12:00–15:30 slot — air-conditioned, ramps/lifts, stroller-friendly, baby-changing facilities.
  - [Pavilhão do Conhecimento](https://www.pavconhecimento.pt/en/visit-us/ticketoffice) (interactive science museum, same Parque das Nações precinct) — hands-on exhibits for ages 5–8.
  - [Telecabine Lisboa cable car](https://www.telecabinelisboa.pt/Prices-Schedules), ~5:30–6pm — seated ride, river breeze, gentle motion suitable for a 5-year-old.
- **Dining:** [Time Out Market Lisboa](https://www.timeout.com/time-out-market-lisboa) (Cais do Sodré) — food-hall format, variety for varying kids' tastes, no fixed-menu wait.
- **Overnight:** [VIP Executive Éden Aparthotel](https://www.viphotels.com/en/hotels/vip-executive-eden-in-lisbon/).

### Day 4 — Tuesday, Aug 4, 2026 — Cascais Day Trip

- **Transport:** [CP suburban train, Cais do Sodré ↔ Cascais](https://www.cascaisportugaltourism.com/transportation/lisbon-to-cascais-trains-from-cais-do-sodre.html), ~33–40 min each way.
- **Activities:**
  - [Praia da Ribeira beach, Cascais](https://www.lisbonbeachesguide.com/cascais/praia-da-ribeira.html), midday (~3–4 hrs) — calm, sheltered, shallow water "ideal for children"; no lifeguard on this beach, direct adult supervision needed.
  - [Cascais old town & marina waterfront stroll](https://www.cascaisportugaltourism.com/transportation/lisbon-to-cascais-trains-from-cais-do-sodre.html), ~4:30–5:30pm, flat pedestrian waterfront, shaded arcades and cafés, before the return train.
- **Dining:** Snacks/light meal in Cascais old town (waterfront cafés) — no specific restaurant recommendation sourced for this stop beyond the old-town stroll note.
- **Overnight:** [VIP Executive Éden Aparthotel](https://www.viphotels.com/en/hotels/vip-executive-eden-in-lisbon/), back in Lisbon.

### Day 5 — Wednesday, Aug 5, 2026 — Lisbon (Departure)

- **Activity:** [São Jorge Castle (Castelo de São Jorge)](https://www.lisbon.net/sao-jorge-castle), ~9:00–10:30am (before peak heat/UV and well before the flight) — taxi/tuk-tuk drop-off near the entrance to minimize the hill climb; flat grounds within the ramparts; panoramic views.
- **Dining:** Light lunch before departure — see general Lisbon dining picks below (e.g. [Stō Restaurante & Mercearia](https://www.eatingeurope.com/blog/family-restaurants-in-lisbon/), near Arco da Rua Augusta) if time permits before the airport transfer.
- **Transport:** [Taxi/Bolt/Uber, Hotel → Airport](https://www.worldtaximeter.com/lisbon/Lisbon+airport/baixa+chiado), ~15–20 min, then [Wizz Air, Lisbon (LIS) → Warsaw Chopin (WAW)](https://us.trip.com/flights/warsaw-to-lisbon/airfares-waw-lis/), nonstop, ~3h50m–4h20m.

- Lightweight breathable tops (adults ×5, children ×6), lightweight shorts/skirts
- 1 lightweight long-sleeve shirt per person (sun protection)
- Sun hats, UV-protective sunglasses (with retainer strap for kids)

- Quick-dry towels, comfortable closed-toe walking sandals/sneakers, flip-flops/pool sandals
- Light pajamas, light cover-up/sarong, compact rain jacket/umbrella (low priority — rain unlikely), swim diapers if needed

- Phone chargers + portable power bank; no plug adapter needed (Portugal shares Poland's Type C/F sockets)

- Kids' tablet/headphones and noise-reducing headphones for the flight

- Valid passport or national ID for each traveler (including children); flight and hotel booking confirmations
- Pre-booked activity tickets (Belém Tower, Jerónimos Monastery, Oceanário, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, Telecabine Lisboa, São Jorge Castle)

**Medicines & Health**
- SPF 50+ sunscreen (full-size, frequent reapplication), after-sun/aloe gel

- Children's fever/pain reducer, insect repellent, motion sickness remedy, reusable insulated water bottles

**Destination-Specific**
- Swim armbands/floaties or puddle jumper for the 5-year-old (no lifeguard at Praia da Ribeira)

## Travel Tips

- **Heat and UV:** Daily highs of 30–34°C and a "very high" to "extreme" UV index (7.65–8.2) are expected. Reapply SPF 50+ sunscreen every 2 hours, use hats and light long sleeves at midday, and favor early morning/late afternoon for outdoor sightseeing — the itinerary is already built around this.
- **Minimal-walking constraint:** Taxi/Bolt/Uber is used in preference to the metro for most transfers (airport and in-city) given two young children and the heat; no day requires an extended walking tour between dispersed sights.
- **Dietary/accessibility:** No dietary constraints or accessibility needs were stated for this trip; all dining and lodging picks reflect that (no allergen filtering applied, no step-free-access filtering applied).
- **Planning estimates to reconfirm at booking:** the flight fare (~€230/person round trip) and the hotel rate (~€190/night) are both planning estimates pending date-specific reconfirmation — even the worst-case scenario still leaves an 18% budget buffer, but reconfirm both closer to booking.
- **Local-transport allowance:** the €150–€200 local-transport estimate is a planning placeholder (midpoint €175 used in the budget total above); actual taxi/transit mix may vary slightly day to day.
- **Cascais beach supervision:** Praia da Ribeira has no lifeguard on duty — direct adult supervision is needed, and the sea temperature (~18–19°C) is cool for children, so rash guards/wetsuit tops are recommended.
- **Adults-only note:** Ginjinha (sour-cherry liqueur) tasting is a fun quick stop for the adults near Rossio Square but is not intended for the children.
