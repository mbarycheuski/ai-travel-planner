# Packing

## Weather Outlook

Source/method: **Open-Meteo seasonal ensemble forecast** (50-member `seasonal_forecast` endpoint), used because the trip dates (~20 days out from the July 12, 2026 planning date) exceed the 16-day deterministic forecast window — per `trips/lisbon-2026-08/weather.md`. Figures below are the ensemble-mean daily highs/lows; day-to-day values carry more uncertainty than a short-range forecast would.

- **Aug 1 (Thu):** 18–27°C, 0 mm rain, wind ~30 km/h. Warm and windy — comfortable for outdoor sightseeing but exposed areas (riverfront, viewpoints) will feel breezy; a light layer helps.
- **Aug 2 (Fri):** 17–26°C, 0 mm rain, wind ~29 km/h. Similar — mild-warm and still windy; favors shaded/indoor time.
- **Aug 3 (Sat):** 18–29°C, 0 mm rain, wind ~24 km/h. Warming trend, beach day — sun exposure is the main risk, not rain.
- **Aug 4 (Sun):** 19–35°C, 0 mm rain, wind ~19 km/h. Peak-heat day — heat/hydration/sun protection are the dominant concern; indoor AC time planned for midday.
- **Aug 5 (Mon):** 21–36°C, 0 mm rain, wind ~10 km/h light. Hottest day of the trip and a travel day — dress for heat from the first hour, pack cooling items accessible (not checked).

**Overall implication:** Dry the entire trip (0 mm forecast every day per the ensemble mean) — no rain gear needed beyond a token compact option. Heat and sun exposure, not rain, drive this list, intensifying sharply on Days 4–5. Wind on Days 1–2 argues for a light layer against breeze despite the heat.

## Clothing

- [ ] Lightweight, breathable, light-colored tops (5–6 per person; quick-dry fabric preferred for the 2 children)
- [ ] Shorts / lightweight trousers or skirts (3–4 per person)
- [ ] 1 light long-sleeve layer per person (for wind on Days 1–2 and sun coverage on peak-heat Days 4–5)
- [ ] Swimwear — at least 2 sets per person (rooftop pool at the hotel + Costa da Caparica beach day; a second set lets one dry while the other is worn)
- [ ] Quick-dry beach towel or two (hotel pool towels are likely provided, but a packable one is useful for the beach day)
- [ ] Wide-brimmed sun hats or caps for all 4 travelers (explicitly noted for Day 5's peak-heat castle visit in `weather.md`)
- [ ] Sunglasses (adult and child-sized, with a strap/retainer for the 5-year-old)
- [ ] Comfortable closed walking sandals or breathable trainers (short walking legs planned, per `activities.md`'s low-exertion pacing)
- [ ] Flip-flops/pool shoes for the rooftop pool and beach
- [ ] Light pajamas/sleepwear for both children
- [ ] Underwear/socks for 5 days + 1 spare set per child (accidents/pool changes)
- [ ] Light cardigan or wrap for air-conditioned museums/restaurants (indoor AC noted at MAAT, Oceanário, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, and the hotel)
- [ ] Compact packable rain layer (very low priority — 0 mm forecast all 5 days, but small enough to include as a token backup)

## Electronics

- [ ] Phone chargers (1 per adult; EU/Portugal uses the same two-round-pin plug as Poland — no adapter needed)
- [ ] Portable power bank (for the beach day and castle visit, where outlets won't be available)
- [ ] Camera or spare phone storage (castle, aquarium, beach photos)
- [ ] Headphones for each child (flight to/from Warsaw, and downtime during Day 4–5 midday AC rest periods)
- [ ] Waterproof phone pouch (for the beach sessions on Day 3)
- [ ] Tablet pre-loaded with offline shows/games for children (useful during the mandated midday indoor-rest blocks on Days 3–5)

## Travel Documents

- [ ] Valid passport or national ID card for each family member (Poland and Portugal are both EU/Schengen members; a national ID card is sufficient — no visa required for this trip) — see Sources
- [ ] Children's ID cards/passports (each child needs their own document, even if listed on a parent's passport)
- [ ] Flight booking confirmations (Warsaw–Lisbon, per `requirements.md`'s confirmed flight transport mode)
- [ ] Hotel booking confirmation (NH Collection Lisboa Liberdade, Family Interconnecting Room, Aug 1–5)
- [ ] Printed/digital copies of activity bookings (Lisboa Story Centre, Jerónimos Monastery, Oceanário, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, Castelo de São Jorge)
- [ ] Travel insurance documents (recommended for the family, incl. child coverage)
- [ ] European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or Polish equivalent for each family member (covers state healthcare in Portugal as a fellow EU state)
- [ ] Copy of accommodation and activity confirmations stored offline (screenshot or printed) in case of connectivity issues

## Medicines & Health

- [ ] High-SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+, kid-safe formula) — the dominant theme across all 5 days per `weather.md`; reapply frequently, especially Day 3 (beach) and Days 4–5 (peak heat 35–36°C)
- [ ] After-sun / aloe vera gel (for both adults and children in case of sunburn)
- [ ] Reusable water bottles for all 4 (refill throughout the day — hydration explicitly flagged for Days 3–5 in `weather.md`)
- [ ] Oral rehydration sachets or electrolyte tablets (for children, given the 35–36°C peak-heat days)
- [ ] Children's fever/pain reducer (paracetamol/ibuprofen, weight-appropriate dosing) — heat-related discomfort is a real risk on Days 4–5
- [ ] Basic first-aid kit (plasters, antiseptic wipes, blister plasters for the light walking legs)
- [ ] Any regular family medications, with a spare few days' supply
- [ ] Insect repellent (light use for the beach/evening riverfront stroll on Day 1)
- [ ] Motion sickness remedy for children if needed for the flight or ferry/bus to Costa da Caparica
