# Packing

## Weather Outlook

Per `weather.md` (Open-Meteo): **Aug 1–3** sourced from the Open-Meteo 14-day deterministic forecast (high confidence); **Aug 4–5** sourced from the Open-Meteo seasonal ensemble model (moderate confidence, 50-member statistical distribution). No rain-gear needed — precipitation is negligible across all five days.

- **Lisbon city center (Aug 1–5):** Daily highs 30–34°C, lows 17–21°C. Clear skies, very high/extreme UV index (7.65–8.2). Virtually no rain (0–6% chance, trace amounts at most). Implication: peak summer heat and severe sunburn risk during 11am–5pm — pack lightweight, breathable, sun-protective clothing and plan for heavy sunscreen use rather than heavy layers.
- **Cascais/Estoril (Day 4 beach day, Aug 4):** Highs 28–32°C (cooler, sea-breeze-moderated vs. the city), lows 17–19°C. Sea temperature ~18–19°C — bracingly cool for young children. Reflective glare off sand/water intensifies UV exposure even with the breeze. Implication: pack rash guards/wetsuit tops for the kids, extra swimwear, and reef-safe sunscreen for a multi-hour midday beach window.
- **Evenings (all stops, ~18–21°C):** Comfortable for outdoor dining or a stroll; a light layer is enough, no warm jacket needed.

**Assumption:** Weather outlook reflects early-August climatology and the specific Aug 1–5, 2026 forecast/seasonal blend recorded in `weather.md`; no rain gear is packed on that basis, but the forecast should be rechecked closer to departure for any late change.

## Clothing

- [ ] Lightweight, breathable short-sleeve tops/T-shirts (2 adults × ~5, 2 children × ~6 — kids get messier/sweatier)
- [ ] Lightweight shorts/skirts (adults) and shorts (children) — enough for 5 days, laundry not assumed
- [ ] 1 lightweight long-sleeve shirt per person (sun protection for midday outdoor stretches, e.g. São Jorge Castle grounds, Cascais old town stroll)
- [ ] Sun hats / wide-brim hats for both children (non-negotiable given "extreme" UV) and adults
- [ ] Sunglasses (UV-protective) for all 4 travelers, with a retainer strap for the kids
- [ ] Swimwear — at least 2 sets per person (1 for the hotel rooftop pool, 1 for the Cascais beach day, so one can dry while the other is worn)
- [ ] Rash guards / swim shirts for both children (extra sun protection for the pool and the notably cool ~18–19°C Cascais sea water)
- [ ] Quick-dry beach towels or a compact microfiber towel per person (hotel pool towels may be provided — confirm at check-in; bring a spare for the Cascais day trip regardless)
- [ ] Comfortable closed-toe walking sandals or lightweight sneakers (for Belém Tower, São Jorge Castle, Telecabine boarding)
- [ ] Flip-flops/pool sandals for the rooftop pool and beach
- [ ] Light pajamas/sleepwear (evenings cool only to ~18–21°C — no need for warm sleepwear)
- [ ] Light cover-up or sarong for after the pool/beach
- [ ] 1 packable light rain jacket or compact umbrella (low priority — precipitation chance is near zero per weather.md, but useful as a minimal contingency)
- [ ] Swim diapers if still relevant for either child (not assumed needed given ages 5/8, but flagged in case)

## Electronics

- [ ] Phone chargers (2, one per adult) and a portable power bank for day-trip use
- [ ] EU-standard plug — Portugal uses the same Type C/F sockets as Poland, so **no plug adapter is needed**
- [ ] Camera or extra phone storage for sightseeing days (Belém Tower, São Jorge Castle, Telecabine cable car, aquarium)
- [ ] Waterproof phone pouch for the Cascais beach day
- [ ] Kids' tablet/headphones for the ~4-hour flight and any downtime
- [ ] Noise-reducing headphones for children (flight comfort)

## Travel Documents

- [ ] Valid passport or national ID card for each traveler — Poland and Portugal are both Schengen/EU states, so an ID card or passport is sufficient (no visa required); carrying one is still strongly recommended for air travel and airline check-in even though it is not strictly checked at the land/Schengen border (see Sources)
- [ ] Children's ID cards or passports (each child needs their own valid document, even if traveling with parents)
- [ ] Flight booking confirmation (Wizz Air, WAW–LIS round trip per `transport.md`)
- [ ] Hotel booking confirmation (VIP Executive Éden Aparthotel, Aug 1–5 per `accommodation.md`)
- [ ] Printed/digital copies of activity tickets pre-booked online (Belém Tower, Jerónimos Monastery, Oceanário de Lisboa, Pavilhão do Conhecimento, Telecabine Lisboa, São Jorge Castle — per `activities.md`)
- [ ] European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or Polish equivalent for each traveler, for reciprocal EU healthcare access
- [ ] Private travel insurance documents (recommended, especially covering two young children)
- [ ] Copy of accommodation address and any pre-booked local transport passes (Navegante 24h combined pass, per `transport.md`)

## Medicines & Health

- [ ] SPF 50+ broad-spectrum sunscreen (sufficient supply for daily reapplication every 2 hours per `weather.md`'s UV guidance — a full-size tube/bottle per adult and child, not just travel-size)
- [ ] After-sun / aloe vera gel for both children in case of sunburn
- [ ] Children's oral rehydration sachets or electrolyte packets (heat exhaustion risk flagged in `weather.md` for sustained outdoor exposure)
- [ ] Any regular prescription medication for all 4 travelers, in original packaging with a spare day's supply
- [ ] Basic first-aid kit: plasters/band-aids, antiseptic wipes, blister plasters (for new sandals), tweezers
- [ ] Children's fever/pain reducer (age-appropriate paracetamol/ibuprofen) in case of heat-related discomfort
- [ ] Insect repellent (light coverage recommended for the Cascais waterfront/evening outdoor dining)
- [ ] Motion sickness remedy for the Telecabine cable car or flight, if either child is prone to it
- [ ] Reusable water bottles for all 4 travelers (insulated, to keep water cool given the 30–34°C city highs) — refill frequently given the heat/hydration risk flagged in `weather.md`

## Destination-Specific

- [ ] Swim armbands/floaties or a puddle jumper for the 5-year-old — Cascais's Praia da Ribeira has no lifeguard on duty per `activities.md`, so parents must supervise directly
- [ ] Small beach bag/day pack for the Cascais day trip (separate from main luggage, per `activities.md`'s Day 4 beach outing)
- [ ] Light backpack or day bag for city sightseeing days (water bottles, sunscreen, snacks, hats)
- [ ] Stroller or lightweight buggy for the 5-year-old if used at home — several venues in `activities.md` are explicitly noted as stroller-friendly (Oceanário, Jerónimos cloisters, Praça do Comércio), though most days favor taxis over long walks
- [ ] Reusable shopping/laundry bag for wet swimwear after the pool or beach (hotel apartment has a kitchenette but laundry facilities are not confirmed in `accommodation.md` — pack enough clothing for the full 4 nights rather than assuming laundry access)
- [ ] Small day-trip cooler bag or insulated pouch for snacks/water on the Cascais train ride (~33–40 min each way per `transport.md`)
- [ ] Euro cash/cards — no packing item, but note as a reminder for the Cascais beach's cash-preferred sunbed/parasol rental (per `activities.md`)
