🗓️ Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1 — Friday, 17.07.2026 — Łódź → Krakow

  • MorningRental car pickup in Łódź, then drive Łódź → Kraków via A1 → S1 → A4 (≈260 km, ≈3h 10m, within the 4-hour cap) — route via conadrogach.pl, tolls per the Stalexport A4 toll price list
  • AfternoonMain Market Square (Rynek Główny) — free walk past the Cloth Hall, Adam Mickiewicz monument, pigeons and horse-drawn cabs (~1 h)
  • AfternoonCloth Hall (Sukiennice) — browse the historic indoor market stalls (~30–45 min)
  • AfternoonSt. Mary's Basilica (Kościół Mariacki) — Gothic interior and Veit Stoss altarpiece (~30–45 min)
  • AfternoonRynek Underground Museum — interactive archaeological/multimedia exhibition beneath the Main Square, family ticket (~1.5–2 h)
  • LunchMilkbar Tomasza — Polish comfort food / milk bar, try the pierogi
  • DinnerPod Wawelem Kompania Kuflowa — traditional Polish beer-hall fare with a kids' play area
  • EveningTreat at Good Lood artisan ice cream, then overnight at Hotel Wielopole — 3★ family room, 5–7 min walk from the Main Square

Day 2 — Saturday, 18.07.2026 — Krakow → Łódź

  • BreakfastLight bakery/café stop (or hotel breakfast, if included)
  • MorningWawel Royal Castle Hill grounds & Arcaded Courtyard — free outdoor walk with hilltop viewpoints (~45–60 min)
  • MorningDragon's Den (Smocza Jama) — legendary cave beneath Wawel Hill, exiting at the fire-breathing dragon statue (~30–40 min)
  • MiddayVistula Boulevards (Bulwary Wiślane) — riverside promenade with playgrounds and open lawns (~45–60 min)
  • MiddayKraków Zoo — family ticket, animal enclosures and playgrounds (~2–2.5 h)
  • LunchMini-Bar Endzior (Pl. Nowy 4B, Kazimierz) — zapiekanka, quick and shareable before the drive home
  • EveningDrive Krakow → Łódź via A4 → S1 → A1 (≈260 km, ≈3h 10m, within the 4-hour cap) — route via conadrogach.pl, then rental car return in Łódź

💡 Travel Tips

  • Accessibility & Dietary: No accessibility needs and no dietary constraints were stated for this trip; activities and dining were chosen without those restrictions in mind.
  • Parking: The hotel does not have its own car park — parking uses the Old Town car park noted above, already folded into the transport total (no double-counting).
  • Basilica Tickets: St. Mary's Basilica ticket pricing assumes the 5-year-old enters free and the 8-year-old pays the reduced (ages 7–18) rate; worth reconfirming at the door.
  • Breakfast: Day 2 breakfast is budgeted as a light bakery/café stop in case it isn't included with the room — treat as a buffer if breakfast turns out to be included.
  • Sun Protection: Both days are warm and dry per the forecast; sun protection (hats, sunscreen, sunglasses) and extra hydration for the children are worth prioritizing, especially for the Saturday Zoo visit (warmest part of the trip).
  • Driving: Both driving legs run comfortably under the 4-hour one-way limit, so no intermediate overnight stop is required.
