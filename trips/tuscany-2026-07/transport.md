## Local Transport (Parking & ZTL per Stop)

- **Florence (start/end)**: Historic center is a full **ZTL** (Zona a Traffico
  Limitato), camera-enforced Mon–Sat daytime/evening; unauthorized entry draws
  fines. Most rental desks are located just outside the ZTL near Santa Maria
  Novella station (Borgo Ognissanti area) or at the airport (~20 min from center
  by tram/car/taxi) — get precise ZTL-avoidance directions from the rental desk
  at pickup, and note the license plate should be registered with the rental
  company/hotel if any ZTL entry is unavoidable for drop-off.
  [The Florence Insider — car rental tips](https://theflorenceinsider.com/car-rental-florence-italy/),
  [Tripadvisor — ZTL rental car return](https://www.tripadvisor.com/ShowTopic-g187895-i68-k10868223-ZTL_Zone_Traffic_Limited_Rental_Car_Return-Florence_Tuscany.html)
- **Greve in Chianti**: No strict ZTL enforcement issue for visitors staying
  centrally; town has public car parks on Via Rosa Libri and Via Luca Cini just
  outside the small historic core. Walkable town square (Piazza Matteotti);
  hotel on-site parking (per accommodation.md requirement) avoids any ZTL
  question entirely. [To-Tuscany — Greve guide](https://www.to-tuscany.com/travel-guide/towns-villages/greve-in-chianti/)
- **Siena**: Historic center (where Piazza del Campo and the Duomo sit) is
  almost entirely **ZTL**, camera-enforced, fines are steep — do not drive in
  without a permit. Use the ring of paid car parks just outside the walls:
  **Il Campo** (near Porta Tufi, closest to Piazza del Campo), **Fortezza-
  Stadio** (~800 spaces), or **San Francesco** (escalator into the center).
  Cheapest option for a full day is the **train station car park** with a
  people-mover/escalator to Porta Camollia. Confirm the hotel's own parking
  (per accommodation.md's on-site parking requirement) is outside the ZTL
  boundary — most Siena hotels with parking are.
  [Visit Siena — official car parks](https://www.visitsiena.it/en/car-parks-in-siena/),
  [Enjoy Tuscany — Siena ZTL/parking guide](https://www.enjoytuscany.it/en/blog/getting-around-siena-tips-for-driving-ztl-parking-alternatives-392)
- **Pienza / Val d'Orcia**: Small town, no strict ZTL issue for tourist traffic;
  park at the free/paid lots just outside the town walls (short walk to the
  main corso) and use hotel on-site parking as base. Val d'Orcia countryside
  excursions (per route.md's outdoor day) require the car — good walkability
  within Pienza itself once parked.
- **San Gimignano**: Strict, permanently active **ZTL** covering the entire
  walled historic center, 24/7 camera enforcement, fines start at ~€90. Must
  park outside the walls at official lots: **P1 Giubileo** (315 spaces, €2/hr
  first 2 hrs, ~5 min walk to Porta San Giovanni) or **P2 Montemaggio** (155
  spaces, €3/hr first hour, ~8 min uphill walk). If the hotel needs closer
  drop-off access, contact them ≥48h ahead to register the car for a temporary
  ZTL exemption. Town itself is fully walkable/pedestrian once parked.
  [TourismAttractions — San Gimignano parking 2026](https://tourismattractions.net/italy/parking-san-gimignano-visitors),
  [TyreMap — San Gimignano ZTL fines](https://tyremap.com/emission-zones/san-gimignano/)

**Assumption**: parking fees at the ZTL-adjacent public lots (Siena, San
Gimignano) are treated as incidental local-transport costs (est. €10–15/day
each at Siena and San Gimignano) and are not folded into the primary rental/
fuel total above; the budget agent should add a small local-parking line item
(~€30–40 total) if hotels don't already include free town-edge parking.

## Rationale

- **Self-drive car** directly matches the confirmed requirement and is the only
  practical way to link the four countryside/small-town stops (Greve, Pienza,
  San Gimignano) — none are well served by direct public transit on this loop.
- **Compact SUV/minivan** sized for 2 adults + 2 children + luggage without
  over-spending on a larger van; keeps rental cost low relative to the €2,500
  all-in budget cap.
- **All legs toll-free** — Tuscany's SR/SP regional road network and the
  Siena–Florence raccordo require no autostrada tolls, which keeps the
  transport line item well under budget and leaves more of the €2,500 cap for
  accommodation, food, and activities.
- **ZTL awareness baked in per stop** — every one of the four historic-center
  towns on this route (Siena, San Gimignano, and to a lesser extent Florence)
  enforces camera-monitored ZTLs; flagging exact park-and-walk points avoids
  fines (which would otherwise blow the budget) and keeps the family's daily
  routine simple (park once at the hotel or a walls-adjacent lot, walk
  everywhere in-town).
- **Drive-time cap honored**: no local-transport choice conflicts with route.md's
  QG2 driving-time cap; local parking walks (5–15 min) are the only "extra"
  walking added, consistent with kid-appropriate pacing (requirements.md).
