# Activities — Tuscany 2026-07 (5-Day Loop, Florence → Florence)

Built from `route.md`'s day/stop structure (Florence → Greve in Chianti → Siena →
Pienza → San Gimignano → Florence). One kid-friendly item is guaranteed on every
day (QG3); culture and outdoor activities are alternated to match the route's
"culture/outdoor balance" (Chianti/Val d'Orcia = outdoor-leaning days, Siena/San
Gimignano = culture-leaning days); no attraction repeats across days (QG6).

## Assumptions (explicit)
- Prices are per-person adult/child rates at 2026 published or most-recently-quoted
  rates; children under 6 are free at nearly all sites, consistent with the trip's
  6- and 9-year-old travelers (the 9-year-old may be charged the 6–17 child rate
  where applicable — flagged per item).
- "Est. cost" for a family of 4 (2 adults + 2 kids, ages 6 & 9) is given as a single
  total per item where child pricing is known; free/self-guided items are marked €0.
- Siena's Torre del Mangia was reported as **temporarily closed** in current
  sourcing ([TripHobo](https://www.triphobo.com/places/siena-italy/torre-del-mangia)) —
  substituted with Orto de' Pecci (free farm/garden) as the Siena outdoor/kid
  anchor rather than risk booking a closed attraction; if reopened by travel date,
  it's an easy swap-in (well-suited to the 9-year-old, less to a 6-year-old given
  the ~400-step climb).
- Driving-day activities (Day 1 arrival, Day 5 departure) are kept light/short per
  route.md's kid-pacing rationale (no full-day sightseeing stacked on a travel day).

## Activities by Stop/Day

### Day 1 — Florence (arrival) → Greve in Chianti
Short arrival day; route.md leaves the afternoon free for gentle exploring.

1. **Piazza Matteotti & Greve historic center stroll** — Culture/leisure — ~45 min
   — €0 — Family suitability: excellent for both kids, flat, stroller-friendly
   central piazza with shops and gelato stops. [The Tuscan Mom](https://thetuscanmom.com/greve-in-chianti-with-kids/)
2. **Chianti Sculpture Park (Pievasciata, ~15 min from Greve)** — Outdoor/art —
   ~1.5–2 h — €10 adult / €5 under-16 → family total ≈ €30 (2 adults + 1 child at
   €5; 6-year-old free under 4? no — under-16 rate applies to both kids here) ≈
   **€30** (2×€10 + 2×€5) — Family suitability: forested walking trail with
   interactive sculptures kids can touch and a picnic amphitheater; flat gravel
   paths, manageable for a 6-year-old with some adult carrying if tired.
   [WhichMuseum](https://whichmuseum.com/museum/chianti-sculpture-park-castelnuovo-berardenga-47766/tickets-prices)

Day 1 total: **€30**

### Day 2 — Greve in Chianti → Siena
Culture day per route.md; midday drive keeps the afternoon for sightseeing.

1. **Piazza del Campo (free roam + fountain/people-watching)** — Culture/outdoor
   — ~1 h — €0 — Family suitability: iconic shell-shaped square, flat brick
   surface, wide open space where kids can run; a natural rest/picnic stop.
   [Visit Tuscany / Opera Duomo Siena](https://operaduomo.siena.it/en/visiting/)
2. **Siena Cathedral (Duomo) ground-floor visit** — Culture — ~45–60 min —
   Adult €5 each (peak-season rate Jan–Jun 26 / Aug 1–17 / Oct–Dec), children
   ≤6 free, 9-year-old ~€5 reduced-est → **≈€15** for the family — Family
   suitability: striking striped marble interior holds younger kids' attention
   briefly; stroller access limited inside, short visit recommended for a
   6-year-old's attention span. [Opera Duomo Siena](https://operaduomo.siena.it/en/visiting/)
3. **Orto de' Pecci (urban farm & garden, ~5 min walk from Piazza del Campo)** —
   Outdoor/kid-friendly — ~1–1.5 h — €0 entry (café/snacks extra) — Family
   suitability: excellent for both ages — donkeys, goats, ducks, and peacocks in
   an open garden setting; flat paths, shaded seating, ideal decompression after
   the Duomo. [Visit Tuscany](https://www.visittuscany.com/en/attractions/orto-de-pecci/), [Hotel Minerva Siena](https://www.albergominerva.it/en/the-amusing-orto-de-pecci-in-siena-for-families/)

Day 2 total: **≈€15**

### Day 3 — Siena → Pienza (Val d'Orcia)
Outdoor/countryside day per route.md; short drive, low-key pacing.

1. **Pienza historic center walk (car-free, flat, playground at the main gate)**
   — Culture/leisure — ~1 h — €0 — Family suitability: ideal for both kids —
   traffic-free, flat streets, playground right at the town gate for a break.
   [Tripadvisor](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Attractions-g644280-Activities-zft11306-Pienza_Tuscany.html)
2. **Cheese-making farm visit near Pienza (e.g., Famiglia Armellini pecorino
   farm)** — Culture/outdoor (agritourism) — ~1.5 h — ≈€10–15/adult, kids often
   free or reduced at small family farms → est. **€40** for the family (typical
   small-group farm-tour + tasting rate; confirm locally as pricing varies by
   farm) — Family suitability: excellent — kids meet farm animals (donkeys,
   goats) and see cheese-making hands-on; vegetarian-friendly tasting (pecorino,
   olive oil, bread). [Mama Loves Italy](https://mamalovesitaly.com/visiting-valdorcia-with-kids/)
3. **Bagno Vignoni free thermal park (Parco dei Mulini, ~10 min drive from
   Pienza)** — Outdoor — ~1 h — €0 (free natural park; full immersion bathing in
   the lower pools is prohibited/finable — foot-soaking in the channels only) —
   Family suitability: good for both kids as a foot-soak/paddle stop; supervise
   closely near water and uneven stone edges (accessibility note: cobbled,
   sloped paths, not stroller-friendly). [Agriturismo La Bruciata](https://www.agriturismolabruciata.it/en/family-holidays/free-natural-hot-springs-in-vignoni.html)

Day 3 total: **≈€40**

### Day 4 — Pienza → San Gimignano
Culture day per route.md; longest drive of the trip, so on-arrival activities
are kept easygoing.

1. **San Gimignano historic center & towers skyline walk** — Culture/outdoor —
   ~45 min — €0 — Family suitability: photogenic medieval streets; some
   cobblestones and gentle slopes, manageable with a stroller-averse but
   walkable 6-year-old.
2. **Torre Grossa (Palazzo Comunale tower climb)** — Culture — ~45 min (218
   steps) — €13 individual entry, or San Gimignano Pass €15 adult/€12 child
   (6–17) covering 2 days & multiple sites → est. **≈€40** for the family (2
   adults + 1 child pass, 6-year-old free) — Family suitability: better suited
   to the 9-year-old; the climb (218 steps, narrow stairs) is demanding for a
   6-year-old — plan for one adult to wait below with the younger child if
   needed (accessibility note: no elevator). [GetYourGuide](https://www.getyourguide.com/great-tower-l168237/), [Pro Loco San Gimignano](https://www.sangimignano.com/en/useful-information/opening-times-and-tickets/)
3. **Gelateria Dondoli (world-champion gelato) stop** — Kid-friendly treat/leisure
   — ~20 min — ≈€3–4/cone → **≈€14** for the family — Family suitability:
   perfect quick win for both kids after the tower climb; vegetarian-friendly.

Day 4 total: **≈€54**

### Day 5 — San Gimignano → Florence (departure)
Short return drive per route.md, leaving time for a final light Florence stop
before departure.

1. **Boboli Gardens (Florence)** — Outdoor/culture — ~1.5 h — Adult ticket
   ≈€10, children 6–17 free with booking, under-6 free → est. **≈€20** for the
   family (2 adult tickets) — Family suitability: excellent for both kids —
   open lawns, duck ponds, and wide gravel paths; stroller-accessible on main
   paths (some garden areas are steeper/gravel). [Uffizi Galleries / CoopCulture](https://www.coopculture.it/en/products/boboli-gardens-entrance-ticket/)
2. **Ponte Vecchio walk + "Il Porcellino" bronze boar fountain stop** — Culture/
   kid-friendly — ~20 min — €0 — Family suitability: great low-effort finale
   for both kids; touching the boar's snout and dropping a coin is a fun,
   free ritual. [The Tuscan Mom](https://thetuscanmom.com/florence-with-kids/)

Day 5 total: **≈€20**

## Rationale

- **Kid-friendly item every day (QG3)**: each day includes at least one
  activity explicitly called out as suitable for a 6-year-old (Chianti
  Sculpture Park trail, Orto de' Pecci animals, Pienza playground + farm
  animals, Gelateria Dondoli, Boboli Gardens duck ponds/lawns).
- **Culture/outdoor balance**: Day 1 (outdoor-leaning: sculpture park), Day 2
  (culture-leaning: Duomo, softened with a free garden/animal stop), Day 3
  (outdoor-leaning: farm + thermal park), Day 4 (culture-leaning: tower +
  historic center), Day 5 (mixed: garden + bridge walk) — alternates rather
  than clustering either theme, consistent with route.md's stated balance.
- **No duplicates (QG6)**: each attraction/site appears exactly once across the
  five days; no town or landmark is revisited.
- **Kid-appropriate pacing**: travel days (1 and 5) carry the lightest
  itineraries (1–2 short items, no big-ticket museum climbs), matching
  route.md's pacing rationale that arrival/return days shouldn't stack a long
  drive with heavy sightseeing. Day 4's tower climb is the single most
  physically demanding item in the trip and is flagged with an alternative
  (one parent waits with the 6-year-old) to avoid forcing a hard climb on the
  younger child.
- **Accessibility notes** are called out per item where relevant (stroller
  access, cobblestones, step counts, water-safety near Bagno Vignoni).
- **Vegetarian dietary note**: farm-visit tasting (pecorino, olive oil, bread)
  and gelato are vegetarian-friendly, aligning with the confirmed vegetarian
  child; detailed dinner planning remains the `food` agent's responsibility.
- **Assumption flagged**: Torre del Mangia (Siena) was reported temporarily
  closed in current sourcing, so Orto de' Pecci was used instead as Siena's
  kid/outdoor anchor — if Torre del Mangia reopens before the trip, it is a
  reasonable swap for families with older kids only (steep 400-step climb),
  but Orto de' Pecci is kept as the primary recommendation for both ages.
