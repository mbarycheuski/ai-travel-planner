# Packing Checklist — Tuscany, July 2026

**Trip profile:** 5-day self-drive loop from/to Florence; 2 adults + 2 children (ages 6, 9); culture (cities, churches, museums) + outdoor activities (walking, countryside, possibly pool/swimming).

**Climate assumption (explicit):** Tuscany in July is hot and dry/sunny — average highs ~28–31°C (82–88°F), lows ~18°C (64°F), only ~3 rainy days/month, sea/pool water ~24°C. Peak sun and peak tourist season. Source: [Tuscany Weather in July – kimkim](https://www.kimkim.com/c/tuscany-in-july-travel-tips-weather-more), [Tuscany July Weather – Weatherspark](https://weatherspark.com/countries/m/IT/16/7). Packing below is tailored for hot, dry, sunny days with cool-ish evenings and a small chance of a summer shower.

---

## Clothing

- Lightweight, breathable tops (cotton/linen) — 1 per person per day
- Shorts / light trousers or skirts — several per person
- 1 modest outfit per person with shoulders and knees covered (for church/cathedral visits — e.g., Duomo Florence, Siena)
- Light sweater or jacket for cool evenings and air-conditioned cars/restaurants
- Swimwear (pool at accommodation and/or countryside swim stops) — 2 sets per person
- Comfortable walking shoes/sneakers (cobblestone streets, hill towns) — broken in, not new
- Sandals for evenings/pool
- Sun hat or cap for each family member, including kids
- Sunglasses (adult and kid sizes)
- Pajamas / sleepwear
- Underwear and socks — enough for trip + 1 spare day (laundry not assumed)
- Light rain jacket or packable poncho (small chance of summer storms)
- Kids: 1 extra full outfit each in the car/day bag (spills, pool splashes)

## Electronics

- Phone chargers (2x, one per adult) + car charger/cable
- Universal/EU plug adapter if coming from non-EU country (Italy uses Type C/F, 230V)
- Portable battery pack (power bank) for day trips
- Car phone mount for navigation
- Camera (optional) + spare memory card/battery
- Headphones/earbuds for kids (car rides, flights)
- Tablet or e-reader loaded with offline kids' shows/games for long down-time and driving legs

## Travel Documents

- Passports (valid 6+ months beyond return date) — all 4 travelers
- Printed + digital copies of flight/rental car/hotel booking confirmations
- International Driving Permit (if required alongside home license — verify requirement for your nationality) + physical driver's license for the driver
- Rental car booking reference and voucher
- Travel insurance policy documents (numbers + emergency contact)
- European Health Insurance Card (EHIC/GHIC) if applicable, or private health insurance card
- Copy of children's IDs/passports and, if traveling without both legal guardians present at any point, a notarized parental consent letter (check requirement based on family situation)
- Credit/debit cards + some cash in euros (€) for tolls, small vendors, parking
- Emergency contact list (embassy, insurance, family back home)

## Medicines & Health

- Any personal prescription medications (enough for full trip + buffer), in original packaging
- Basic first-aid kit: adhesive bandages, antiseptic wipes, blister plasters (for lots of walking), tweezers
- Children's and adult pain/fever reducer (e.g., paracetamol/ibuprofen)
- Motion sickness remedy for kids (winding Tuscan roads)
- High-SPF sunscreen (kid-safe formula) and after-sun/aloe gel — reapply often given July sun intensity
- Insect repellent (mosquitoes common in Tuscan countryside evenings)
- Rehydration salts/electrolyte packets for kids given summer heat
- Hand sanitizer and a few disposable masks
- Reusable water bottles for each family member (refill — Italy has public water fountains)

## Destination-Specific

- Lightweight daypack/backpack for museum and town-walking days
- Small cooler bag or insulated pouch for car (water, snacks between towns given limited stops)
- Coins/small change for parking meters, public restrooms, church donation boxes
- Reusable shopping bag (useful for markets, picnics)
- Picnic set (foldable, for countryside stops — cheese, bread, fruit from local markets)
- Kids' travel activity kit (small toys, coloring book, cards) for driving legs and restaurant waits
- Guidebook or offline map/app for hill towns with limited signal
- Modest cover-up/scarf for each adult (extra layer for church visits beyond the standard outfit)
- Individual small crossbody bag or money belt for valuables in busy tourist areas (Florence, Siena)
- Laundry detergent sheets or travel-size laundry pods (5-day trip — light laundry may be useful given hot weather sweat/pool use)
