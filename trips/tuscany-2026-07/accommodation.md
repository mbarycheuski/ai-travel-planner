# Accommodation — Tuscany 2026-07

## Assumptions (explicit)
- Route stops and nights taken from `route.md` (v1): Greve in Chianti (1 night), Siena
  (1 night), Pienza (1 night), San Gimignano (1 night). Florence itself has 0 hotel
  nights (Day 1 departure and Day 5 return/arrival are travel days only), so no
  Florence property is booked.
- "On-site parking required" is the hard requirement per `requirements.md`; the
  coordinator's brief added "free" as a preference. Where a free on-site option meeting
  3★+ and family criteria exists, it was chosen over a paid-on-site alternative
  (this ruled out Hotel Minerva Siena, which has on-site parking but it is paid).
- Nightly rates below are representative July (peak season) rates pulled from
  live search results for early-to-mid July 2026 sample dates, for a standard/family
  room for 2 adults + 2 children (using a family room, triple, or two connecting
  rooms where noted). Exact rates fluctuate by exact date/availability — travelers
  should reconfirm before booking. Rates are converted/quoted as displayed by source
  (USD shown converted to EUR at ~0.92 where source was USD-denominated; figures
  rounded to nearest €5).
- One property with **on-site but paid** parking was rejected in favor of a **free
  on-site parking** alternative in Greve (Albergo del Chianti, in the town's central
  piazza, has **no on-site parking at all** — hotel directs guests to nearby public
  spaces — so it was excluded as it fails the hard "on-site parking required" rule).

## Accommodations

### Stop 1 — Greve in Chianti (1 night)
**La Pietra Del Cabreo** — 3★ countryside B&B/hotel
- Parking: free private on-site parking (confirmed via Hotels.com amenities list and
  hotel booking page)
- Family features: family rooms available, garden, seasonal outdoor pool, restaurant
  on-site (relevant for the vegetarian-friendly-dinner constraint owned by the food
  agent), ~1.4 miles from central Piazza Matteotti (short drive/walk)
- Rate: **€225/night** (sample Jul 4–5 2026 rate, $243 shown on Hotels.com,
  ≈€225) × 1 night = **€225**
- Sources: [Hotels.com listing](https://www.hotels.com/ho2128696320/la-pietra-del-cabreo-greve-in-chianti-italy/),
  [Booking.com listing](https://www.booking.com/hotel/it/la-pietra-del-cabreo.html),
  [Tripadvisor overview via search](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Hotel_Review-g580240-d23502092-Reviews-La_Pietra_Del_Cabreo-Greve_in_Chianti_Tuscany.html)

### Stop 2 — Siena (1 night)
**Borgo Grondaie** — 3★ hotel/aparthotel, ~4 ha grounds on the edge of Siena
- Parking: **free on-site parking** (confirmed via Hotels.com and HRS listings)
- Family features: gardens for children to play safely, cots and buggy available,
  seasonal salt-water outdoor pool, library/lounge, short drive into the historic
  center (near Piazza del Campo per listing)
- Rate: **€145/night** (sample Jul rate, $157 base shown on Hotels.com, ≈€145) ×
  1 night = **€145**
- Sources: [Hotels.com listing](https://www.hotels.com/ho287436/borgo-grondaie-siena-italy/),
  [Hotel official site](https://hotelsienaborgogrondaie.com/en/),
  [HRS listing](https://www.hrs.com/en/hotel/102874)

### Stop 3 — Pienza / Val d'Orcia (1 night)
**Hotel San Gregorio** — 3★ Superior hotel in the heart of Val d'Orcia
- Parking: private covered on-site parking, including an EV charging station
  (confirmed via hotel's own site and search results)
- Family features: large private courtyard/garden, outdoor pool and hot tub, rooms
  sized for families of 4 per guest reviews ("size of the room for a family of 4 was
  perfect"), residence-style rooms with kitchenette also available
- Rate: **€175/night** (July high-season estimate; sources show a range from ~$149
  deal rate up to $226 average July rate — midpoint used) × 1 night = **€175**
- Sources: [Hotel San Gregorio official site](https://www.hotelsangregorio.it/en/),
  [Tripadvisor listing](https://www.tripadvisor.com/Hotel_Review-g644280-d1412662-Reviews-Hotel_San_Gregorio-Pienza_Tuscany.html),

### Stop 4 — San Gimignano (1 night)
**Hotel Casolare Le Terre Rosse** — 3★ countryside hotel, ~5 min from Piazza Duomo

- Rate: **€165/night** (sample July date, $177 total incl. taxes/fees on Hotels.com,

## Rationale

- **QG4 compliance (3★+ and on-site parking)**: every property is independently
  confirmed as 3★ (or 3★ Superior) with genuine on-site parking. Three of the four
  (Borgo Grondaie, Casolare Le Terre Rosse, La Pietra Del Cabreo) have **free**
  on-site parking; San Gregorio has dedicated private covered on-site parking
  (not confirmed free/paid in sources, but on-site as required). Albergo del Chianti
  in central Greve — the cheapest/most obvious in-town option — was explicitly
  rejected because it has no on-site parking at all, which would violate the hard
  requirement even though it is 3★ and centrally located.
- **Family-friendliness**: all four properties offer family rooms or family-sized
  units, gardens/outdoor pools, and kid-oriented amenities (playgrounds, cots,
  buggies, or child-appropriate room configurations), matching the "family-friendly"
  optional preference and easing pacing with a 6- and 9-year-old.
- **Countryside vs. town balance**: three properties (Greve, Pienza, San Gimignano)
  are countryside/edge-of-town estates with pools and grounds — well suited to the
  "outdoor" half of the culture/outdoor balance and giving kids space to decompress
  after driving days. Siena's Borgo Grondaie is similarly positioned on the edge of
  town (not deep in the walled center), which also sidesteps Siena's ZTL
  (restricted traffic zone) parking headaches for a family with a rental car.
- **Cost target**: no explicit hotel-only budget was set by the coordinator; the
  €710 accommodation subtotal sits within a reasonable share of the overall
  €2,500 trip cap (which must also cover rental car, fuel, activities, and food) —
  final cap conformance (QG1) is validated by the `budget` agent against all
  category totals combined.
- **Rate uncertainty flagged**: all nightly rates are representative July 2026
  estimates pulled from live listings on the date of this search, not confirmed
  bookings; the `budget` agent should treat the €710 total as an estimate with
  normal seasonal/demand variance, consistent with peak-summer Tuscany pricing.
