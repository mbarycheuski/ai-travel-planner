# Food — Tuscany 2026-07 (5-Day Loop, Florence → Florence)

## Assumptions
- Route/towns taken from `route.md`: Florence (Day 1 arrival / Day 5 departure), Greve
  in Chianti (Night 1), Siena (Night 2), Pienza (Night 3), San Gimignano (Night 4).
- One dinner recommendation set is provided per overnight stop (dinner is the meal
  families typically book/plan for); lunches are assumed to be casual/flexible
  (trattorias, pizza-by-the-slice, picnics) and are folded into the daily cost estimate
  rather than individually booked.
- "Vegetarian-friendly" = restaurant has at least one clearly vegetarian main (not just
  a side salad) confirmed via menu/review sources cited below, satisfying the
  requirement that one child (vegetarian) has a real dinner option at every stop.
- Prices are per-person mid-range estimates in EUR, based on cited sources (Tripadvisor,
  TheFork, Wanderlog) as of 2026; actual à-la-carte totals vary with orders and
  drinks. No specific hotel/restaurant address confirmed beyond town center — exact
  booking is left to the traveler closer to the date.
- Florence is included as a bookend for Day 1 (arrival, likely a light lunch/dinner
  before driving to Greve) and Day 5 (departure day, likely a lunch before flight) —
  one option is given for each rather than a full "stop" writeup, since no overnight
  stay occurs there per the route.

## Local Food to Try

- **Chianti (Greve)**: Bistecca alla Fiorentina (T-bone steak, non-veg), pici al ragù,
  crostini di fegatini, local Chianti Classico wine, extra-virgin olive oil tastings.
- **Siena**: Pici cacio e pepe / all'aglione, ribollita (vegetable/bread soup —
  naturally vegetarian if made without lardo), panforte and ricciarelli (almond
  sweets) for dessert/souvenirs.
- **Val d'Orcia / Pienza**: Pecorino di Pienza (sheep's milk cheese, often served with
  honey or pear — vegetarian), cinta senese salumi (non-veg), pappardelle with
  seasonal vegetables/porcini.
- **San Gimignano**: Vernaccia di San Gimignano (local white wine), saffron (grown
  locally, used in risotto), wild boar dishes (non-veg) alongside vegetable-forward
  Tuscan bean dishes (fagioli all'uccelletto).
- **Florence**: Schiacciata (flatbread sandwiches, easy to make vegetarian), gelato —
  widely available and a reliable kid-pleaser at every stop.
