## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1 — Florence (arrival) → Greve in Chianti
- **Route leg**: Florence → Greve in Chianti, ~30 km, **~35–40 min drive** (SR222 "Chiantigiana", no tolls)
- **Activities**: Piazza Matteotti & Greve historic center stroll (~45 min, free); Chianti Sculpture Park, Pievasciata (~1.5–2h, ≈€30/family)
- **Dining suggestion**: Osteria Uscio & Bottega (Tuscan, vegetarian dishes available, ~€35/person) — or Lo Spela Naturalmente Pizza for a casual/vegetarian-pizza backup
- **Night's accommodation**: **La Pietra Del Cabreo** (3★ countryside B&B, free on-site parking) — €225

### Day 2 — Greve in Chianti → Siena
- **Route leg**: Greve in Chianti → Siena, ~42–45 km, **~50 min–1h drive** (regional roads, no tolls)
- **Activities**: Piazza del Campo free roam (~1h, free); Siena Cathedral/Duomo ground-floor visit (~45–60 min, ≈€15/family); Orto de' Pecci urban farm & garden (~1–1.5h, free entry)
- **Dining suggestion**: Tre Cristi Enoteca Ristorante — dedicated vegetarian tasting menu (~€35–45/person); Rusticamente Osteria as a family-friendly alternative
- **Night's accommodation**: **Borgo Grondaie** (3★ hotel/aparthotel, free on-site parking, near the historic center) — €145

### Day 3 — Siena → Pienza (Val d'Orcia)
- **Route leg**: Siena → Pienza, ~55–68 km, **~1h–1h15min drive** (SS2 Cassia / SP roads, no tolls)
- **Activities**: Pienza historic center walk with gate-side playground (~1h, free); cheese-making farm visit near Pienza, e.g. Famiglia Armellini pecorino farm (~1.5h, ≈€40/family); Bagno Vignoni free thermal park / Parco dei Mulini foot-soak (~1h, free)
- **Dining suggestion**: Ristorante Il Rossellino ("plenty of vegetarian food available," ~€25–35/person); La Buca delle Fate or La Combriccola as alternatives
- **Night's accommodation**: **Hotel San Gregorio** (3★ Superior, Val d'Orcia, private covered on-site parking + EV charging) — €175

### Day 4 — Pienza → San Gimignano
- **Route leg**: Pienza → San Gimignano, ~93 km, **~1h40min drive** — longest leg of the trip, still ~50 min under the 2.5h cap (regional roads, no tolls)
- **Activities**: San Gimignano historic center & towers skyline walk (~45 min, free); Torre Grossa tower climb, 218 steps (~45 min, ≈€40/family — best suited to the 9-year-old; consider one adult waiting below with the 6-year-old); Gelateria Dondoli gelato stop (~20 min, ≈€14/family)
- **Dining suggestion**: Portanova Hosteria Enoteca (vegetarian dishes available, ~€25/person); Dietro le Quinte or Milleluci as alternatives
- **Night's accommodation**: **Hotel Casolare Le Terre Rosse** (3★ countryside hotel, free on-site parking, on-site playground) — €165

### Day 5 — San Gimignano → Florence (departure)
- **Route leg**: San Gimignano → Florence, ~55 km, **~55 min–1h drive** (return to rental drop-off point, no tolls)
- **Activities**: Boboli Gardens (~1.5h, ≈€20/family); Ponte Vecchio walk + "Il Porcellino" bronze boar fountain stop (~20 min, free)
- **Dining suggestion**: Nirvana Veg (fully vegetarian menu, €15–20/person) or La Cupola (family-friendly, vegetarian options, €15–25/person)
- **Night's accommodation**: None — departure day (return flight/onward travel)

## Packing Checklist

**Climate**: Tuscany in July is hot and dry — highs ~28–31°C, lows ~18°C, minimal rain, peak sun and tourist season.

**Clothing**
- Lightweight, breathable tops (1/person/day); shorts/light trousers or skirts
- 1 modest outfit per person (shoulders/knees covered) for churches/cathedrals
- Light sweater or jacket for cool evenings / air-conditioned spaces
- Swimwear (2 sets/person); comfortable broken-in walking shoes; sandals for evenings/pool
- Sun hats and sunglasses for all; pajamas; underwear/socks (trip length + 1 spare day)
- Light rain jacket or packable poncho; 1 extra full kids' outfit in the day bag

**Electronics**
- Phone chargers + car charger; EU Type C/F plug adapter if needed; portable battery pack
- Car phone mount for navigation; camera (optional); kids' headphones/earbuds; tablet with offline entertainment for drives

**Travel Documents**
- Passports (valid 6+ months past return) for all 4 travelers; booking confirmations (printed + digital)
- International Driving Permit (if required) + driver's license; rental car voucher
- Travel insurance documents; EHIC/GHIC or private health insurance card
- Children's ID copies + parental consent letter if applicable; credit/debit cards + euro cash; emergency contact list

**Medicines & Health**
- Personal prescriptions (full trip + buffer); basic first-aid kit (bandages, antiseptic, blister plasters, tweezers)
- Children's and adult pain/fever reducer; motion sickness remedy for winding roads
- High-SPF kid-safe sunscreen + after-sun gel; insect repellent; electrolyte packets
- Hand sanitizer; reusable water bottles for each family member

**Destination-Specific**
- Lightweight daypack; small cooler bag for car snacks/water
- Coins/small change for parking meters, restrooms, church donations
- Picnic set for countryside stops; kids' travel activity kit for drives/waits
- Offline maps/guidebook for hill towns with limited signal; modest cover-up/scarf per adult for churches
- Crossbody bag/money belt for valuables in busy areas (Florence, Siena)
- Laundry sheets/pods for a light mid-trip wash

*Packing was compiled independently of the final route; cross-check against Day 3's farm/thermal-park stop and Day 4's tower climb for any extra gear (e.g., closed-toe shoes for the farm, water shoes for Bagno Vignoni).*

## Travel Tips

- **ZTL zones**: Florence, Siena, and San Gimignano all enforce camera-monitored restricted traffic zones in their historic centers. Never drive into these areas without authorization — fines are steep (San Gimignano fines start ~€90). Always park at the hotel's on-site lot or a walls-adjacent public car park and walk in.
- **Driving pace**: Every leg is well under the 2.5h/day cap (longest is Pienza → San Gimignano at ~1h40min), leaving buffer for rest stops and kid bathroom breaks.
- **Vegetarian dining**: A vegetarian-friendly main is confirmed at every overnight stop and the Florence bookend meals — look for pici, ribollita (ask for no lardo), pecorino, and vegetable-forward Tuscan dishes.
- **Kid pacing**: Travel days (Day 1 arrival, Day 5 departure) are intentionally light. Day 4's Torre Grossa climb (218 steps) is the most demanding activity — consider splitting the group if the 6-year-old struggles.
- **Bagno Vignoni safety**: Full immersion in the lower thermal pools is prohibited and finable; foot-soaking in the channels only. Paths are cobbled and sloped — supervise kids closely.
- **Book ahead**: Reserve restaurant tables in Siena and Greve where noted, and confirm hotel rates/on-site parking terms (especially Hotel San Gregorio's free-vs-paid status) before final booking, as all rates are July 2026 estimates subject to change.
- **Sun and heat**: July is peak sun — reapply sunscreen often, carry water, and plan the most strenuous activities (tower climb, farm visit) for morning or late afternoon where possible.
